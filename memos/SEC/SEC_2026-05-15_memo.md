# Senvest Capital Inc. (SEC) — Earnings Memo
Period: Q1 FY2026 | Report Date: 2026-05-15 | Prepared: 2026-07-13
Figures in C$. Listing: TSX. Nature: investment holding company (Senvest-managed funds).

## 1. Snapshot
- "Revenue": total revenue and net investment losses $(74.9)M (vs $(838.2)M PY) — source: Senvest Q1 2026 report, 2026-05-15
- EPS: net loss attributable to common $(20.13)/share (net loss $(48.8)M) — vs $(136.14)/share ($(332.0)M) in Q1 2025
- One-line takeaway: A modestly negative quarter for the investment portfolio — but a dramatic improvement versus last year's catastrophic Q1; book value dipped only slightly (comprehensive loss just $(16.05)M). NAV/book value, not EPS, is the lens.

## 2. What happened this quarter
Senvest is effectively a listed wrapper around the Senvest-managed hedge funds, so its P&L is the mark-to-market of those investments and swings violently quarter to quarter. Q1 2026 produced a net loss attributable to common shareholders of $(48.8)M ($(20.13)/share), driven by net investment losses of ~$(95)M partly offset by income. That's a loss — but the context is everything: the prior-year Q1 2025 was a $(332.0)M loss ($(136.14)/share), so this is a ~85% smaller loss and a sign the portfolio's drawdown was far milder. Notably, the comprehensive loss (which captures FX on foreign holdings) was only $(16.05)M, so book value was close to flat on the quarter.

For Senvest, the single number that matters is book value per unit (NAV). Total equity attributable to common shareholders remained well over C$1.7B against ~2.42M shares, implying a book value in the ballpark of ~C$700+/share [EST — the two-column balance-sheet layout made precise extraction unreliable; confirm from the clean statements on SEDAR+].

## 3. Guidance & management commentary
No guidance (investment holding company). Senvest's "strategy" is the fund managers' positioning — concentrated, activist/value equity bets. The MD&A discusses specific investee companies' prospects (e.g., references to portfolio holdings' future earnings and buybacks), but these are the underlying holdings, not Senvest itself. Tone: as usual, focused on long-term compounding of book value through market cycles.

## 4. Balance sheet & capital allocation
- Total equity attributable to common shareholders ≈ C$1.7B+ [confirm]; the company also consolidates fund structures (redeemable units, securities sold short) that inflate gross balance-sheet line items.
- Senvest has historically been an aggressive repurchaser of its own deeply-discounted shares — worth confirming buyback activity this quarter from the filing [not captured this run].
- Trades at a persistent, wide discount to book value (typical for the structure).

## 5. Thesis check
- Does not change the (long-term) thesis: for a book-value-compounding, discount-to-NAV holding, a small Q1 loss that's a fraction of last year's is neutral-to-mildly-positive. The portfolio held up far better than in 2025.
- Risks: extreme quarter-to-quarter volatility tied to the funds' concentrated positions; the wide discount to book can persist; single-manager/key-person dependence; leverage/short exposure within the consolidated funds.
- Valuation: the metric is price vs. book value per share (~C$700+/share [EST]). Senvest typically trades at a large discount to book — that discount, and book-value growth over cycles, is the return driver. Confirm exact BVPS before acting.
