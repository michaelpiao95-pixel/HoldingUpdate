# International Workplace Group plc (IWG) — Earnings Memo
Period: Q1 FY2026 (trading update) | Report Date: ~2026-05-13 [date approximate] | Prepared: 2026-07-13
Figures in US$. Note: IWG reports full P&L half-yearly; Q1 is a revenue-only trading update.

## 1. Snapshot
- Revenue: system-wide $1,166M (YoY +9%); group revenue $958M (+4%) — source: IWG Q1 2026 trading statement
- Earnings: no EPS in a Q1 trading update; FY2026 adjusted EBITDA guidance $585–625M (vs FY2025 $531M)
- One-line takeaway: Growth on strategy — asset-light managed & franchised revenue surged (+41% system-wide, recurring fee +80%), company-owned steady (+2%, RevPAR +6%), full-year guidance maintained.

## 2. What happened this quarter
IWG's pivot to an asset-light, capital-light model is showing through. Group revenue grew 4% to $958M and system-wide revenue (including partner-operated locations) grew 9% to $1,166M. The standout was the Managed & Franchised segment: system-wide revenue up 41% to $260M, fee revenue of $39M, and recurring fee revenue up 80% to $16M — this is the high-margin, low-capital growth engine IWG has been building toward. Company-owned operations were steadier (revenue +2% to $906M) but with healthy pricing — RevPAR rose 6% to $389 from $340. Network expansion continued at pace (382 signings, 222 openings), following a record 782 openings in FY2025.

Because this is a trading update, there's no profit/EPS detail — the read is that top-line momentum and the mix shift toward fees are on track.

## 3. Guidance & management commentary
Management maintained FY2026 guidance: adjusted EBITDA of $585–625M (up from $531M in FY2025), company-owned revenue growth of at least 4% with roughly flat costs (operating leverage), and recurring managed fee income of $80M. Tone: confident on the asset-light expansion and capital returns. Strategic thrust is unchanged — grow the franchised/managed network (low capital, recurring fees) while running the owned estate for cash.

## 4. Balance sheet & capital allocation
- Net financial debt rose to $858M, attributed to share repurchases and bonus payments — i.e., leverage ticked up partly to fund capital returns.
- Capital returns: $75M returned to shareholders year-to-date — the buyback is a core part of the equity story.
- Watch the balance between buybacks and net debt; IWG carries meaningful lease liabilities inherent to the model.

## 5. Thesis check
- Supports the thesis: the asset-light transition is delivering outsized fee growth (+41% system-wide, +80% recurring fees) and EBITDA guidance steps up materially YoY — the core "capital-light compounding + buybacks" case is intact.
- Risk: office-demand cyclicality, the owned estate's operating leverage cutting both ways, rising net debt, and lease obligations. A trading update also limits visibility into profitability/cash conversion.
- Valuation: not sourced. [EST] Re-rating hinges on the market crediting the recurring-fee mix shift and EBITDA reaching the $585–625M guide; not quantified without a quote.

## 6. Watch items for next period (H1 2026 results, ~Aug 2026)
1. Adjusted EBITDA vs. the $585–625M guide, and cash conversion (the half-year P&L will show it).
2. Managed & Franchised fee growth durability and the $80M recurring-fee target.
3. Net debt trajectory vs. buyback pace.
