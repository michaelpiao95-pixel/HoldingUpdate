# Genie Energy Ltd. (GNE) — Earnings Memo
Period: Q1 FY2026 | Report Date: 2026-05-13 | Prepared: 2026-07-11

## 1. Snapshot
- Revenue: $142.3M (YoY +4%) — source: Genie Energy Q1 2026 release, ~2026-05-13 [via release summary]
- EPS: GAAP diluted $0.11 — net income attributable to common $2.8M (down from $10.4M PY)
- One-line takeaway: Revenue up but profitability collapsed — adjusted EBITDA fell to $2.8M from $14.4M on retail-margin compression and higher spend, and management CUT full-year 2026 EBITDA guidance.

## 2. What happened this quarter
A weak quarter beneath a flat-to-up top line. Revenue rose 4% to $142.3M — driven by retail pricing and higher solar sales — but the profit picture deteriorated sharply: income from operations of just $1.9M (1.3% operating margin), gross margin down to 20.9%, and net income attributable to common of $2.8M ($0.11/sh), less than a third of the prior-year $10.4M. Adjusted EBITDA cratered to $2.8M from $14.4M. Management attributed the compression to weaker retail supply margins, higher customer-acquisition spending, weather-influenced commodity costs, and lower-margin solar inventory.

The read: Genie's core GRE retail-energy margins normalized down hard from an unusually strong prior-year period, while the company is simultaneously investing in customer growth and renewables — a double hit to near-term earnings.

## 3. Guidance & management commentary
The key negative signal: Genie cut full-year 2026 adjusted EBITDA guidance from $40–50M to $32.5–40M, citing Q1 margin compression, higher customer-acquisition costs, and increased renewable-energy investment. Tone: cautious/defensive on near-term margins, while framing customer-acquisition and renewable spend as investments for future growth. Strategic tension: growth spend (customers + solar) vs. defending retail profitability.
