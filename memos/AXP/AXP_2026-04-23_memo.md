# American Express Company (AXP) — Earnings Memo
Period: Q1 FY2026 | Report Date: 2026-04-23 | Prepared: 2026-07-11

## 1. Snapshot
- Revenue: ~$18.9B net of interest expense (YoY +11% reported, +10% FX-adjusted) — source: AXP Q1 2026 release, 2026-04-23
- EPS: GAAP diluted $4.28 (+18% YoY; beat ~$4.00) — net income $2,971M (+15% YoY)
- One-line takeaway: Strong, on-strategy quarter — record-ish spend growth (+10%), revenue +11%, EPS +18%; full-year guidance reaffirmed (revenue +9–10%, EPS $17.30–$17.90).

## 2. What happened this quarter
Amex's premium, spend-driven model kept compounding. Total revenues net of interest expense rose 11% (10% FX-adjusted) to ~$18.9B, and billed business hit $428.0B, up 10% reported (9% FX-adjusted). Card Member spending grew 10% — the highest quarterly growth in three years — with strength in both goods & services and travel & entertainment, signaling the affluent consumer base remains resilient. Net income rose 15% to $3.0B and EPS grew 18% to $4.28, with the ~2% lower share count (686M avg diluted) adding to per-share growth.

Credit stayed a relative strength for Amex's prime book: provisions for credit losses of $1.3B, consistent with contained delinquencies. The quarter reinforced the flywheel — premium card fees + spend growth + disciplined credit.

## 3. Guidance & management commentary
Management reaffirmed full-year 2026 guidance: revenue growth of 9–10% and EPS of $17.30–$17.90. Reaffirming (rather than raising) after an +18% EPS quarter is characteristically conservative and signals confidence in the full-year algorithm without over-promising. Strategically, Amex announced a multiyear NFL partnership (official payments partner from the 2026 season) — a brand/engagement investment aimed at its premium acquisition engine. Tone: confident, steady.

## 4. Balance sheet & capital allocation
- Share count down ~2% YoY (686M avg diluted) — ongoing buybacks supplement per-share growth.
- Specific CET1/buyback/dividend dollar figures not captured in the extracted release text [not captured this run] — Amex funds returns from strong earnings while investing in Card Member value (rewards/marketing).
- No liquidity or credit-reserve flags.

## 5. Thesis check
- Supports the thesis: the premium-spend model is delivering double-digit revenue and EPS growth with contained credit — exactly the algorithm. Reaffirmed guidance underpins the full-year path.
- Risk: the model is levered to affluent-consumer spending (T&E in particular); a consumer slowdown would hit billed business first. Credit normalization to watch, though benign now.
- Valuation: not sourced. [EST] On reaffirmed EPS $17.30–$17.90, the multiple hinges on sustained ~10% spend growth; not quantified without a market quote.

## 6. Watch items for next quarter (Q2 2026, reports ~mid/late-July 2026)
1. Billed business / Card Member spend growth — does +10% hold, especially T&E?
2. Credit metrics (provisions, delinquencies, write-off rate) for any normalization.
3. Progress toward the FY2026 revenue +9–10% / EPS $17.30–$17.90 guide.
