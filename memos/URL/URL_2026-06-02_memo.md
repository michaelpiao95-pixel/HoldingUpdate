## 2. What happened this quarter
NameSilo is one of the world's larger domain registrars, and the domain business is inherently steady (recurring registrations/renewals). Q1 revenue reached a record $16.98M, up 7.0% YoY, driven by more domains under management, higher marketplace revenue, ancillary-service sales, and — notably — contributions from acquired businesses SewerVue Technologies and Reach Systems, reflecting management's diversification beyond pure domains. Gross profit was $4.30M, though gross margin ticked down slightly to 25.3% (from 26.0%), consistent with the low-margin, high-volume registrar model and mix from newer businesses.

Forward indicators were healthy: total bookings of $19.16M (+9.3%) ran ahead of recognized revenue, and deferred revenue grew to $34.74M (from $32.75M at year-end 2025) — the deferred-revenue build is a positive leading signal for a subscription/renewal business.

## 3. Guidance & management commentary
No formal guidance (small-cap CSE issuer). The strategic thread is clear: grow the core registrar while diversifying into adjacent/non-domain businesses via acquisition (SewerVue, Reach Systems), funded from a debt-free balance sheet. Tone read: growth-and-diversification. The open question is whether the acquired non-domain businesses (e.g., SewerVue's pipe-inspection tech) are a coherent capital-allocation strategy or a conglomerate drift.
