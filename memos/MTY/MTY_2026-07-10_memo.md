# MTY Food Group Inc. (MTY) — Earnings Memo
Period: Q2 FY2026 (13 wks ended ~2026-05-31) | Report Date: 2026-07-10 | Prepared: 2026-07-14

## 1. Snapshot
- Revenue: $279.9M (YoY -8.2% from $304.9M; QoQ approx. flat) — source: [GlobeNewswire press release, July 10, 2026]
- EPS: GAAP $0.67 diluted ($15.4M net income) vs. $2.49 ($57.3M) in Q2 FY2025 — impacted by $42.7M FX headwind and $7.5M store-closure impairment
- Adj EBITDA: $59.9M (vs. $69.3M YoY); Normalized adj EBITDA: $60.2M (vs. $70.0M YoY)
- Free cash flow (net of lease payments): $32.2M ($1.41/share)
- One-line takeaway: Revenue and profit miss YoY on consumer softness and FX drag; however, underlying FCF remains solid and management is taking decisive store rationalization action

## 2. What happened this quarter

Revenue declined 8.2% YoY to $279.9M, split across three segments: Franchise ($98.6M, -4%), Corporate ($111.7M, -15%), and Food Processing/Distribution/Retail ($39.3M, -2%). The corporate-owned segment bore the brunt of weaker consumer spending, as franchised royalty streams held up better. MTY's geographic system sales total of $1.4B also reflect the weaker environment — same-store sales declined 2.2% in the U.S., 1.8% in Canada, and 5.2% internationally. Encouragingly, management noted Canadian same-store sales turned positive in June for most concepts, suggesting the trend improved through the quarter.

The dramatic YoY net income collapse from $57.3M to $15.4M is primarily explained by two items: a $42.7M negative foreign exchange variance (C$/US$ translation drag on US-denominated results) and a $7.5M impairment charge for the accelerated closure of 68 underperforming corporate-owned stores over the next nine months. Stripping out FX and one-time charges, underlying operating cash generation remained healthy — operating CF was $43.0M (+25% or +$8.7M YoY), reflecting improved working capital management.

Normalized adj EBITDA of $60.2M ($70.0M in Q2'25) declined $9.8M YoY, consistent with softer top-line and the challenging consumer spending backdrop. Margins are compressed but the royalty-heavy franchise model limits fixed-cost exposure. The shift toward closing 68 underperforming corporate locations (36% of total corporate stores) is an important structural move: it will reduce the higher-volatility corporate revenue stream and improve consolidated margin over the next two to three quarters.

## 3. Guidance & management commentary

MTY does not provide formal quarterly EPS guidance. CEO Eric Lefebvre's commentary struck a cautious but constructive tone:
- Consumer spending pressures acknowledged as a headwind, but Canadian SSS trends improving through the quarter (June positive)
- Decisive action on corporate store portfolio: "closing 68 underperforming corporate-owned stores" — a deliberate capital allocation decision to prioritize profitability over scale
- "Expectations for accelerated development activity in the second half of 2026" — suggests franchisee pipeline remains active
- Dividend maintained at $0.37/share, payable August 14, 2026 — signals confidence in FCF sustainability

No revenue or earnings guidance range was provided. The continuation of the $0.37/share quarterly dividend implies management sees FCF coverage as adequate (FCF of $32.2M vs. quarterly dividend cost of ~$8.5M based on ~23M diluted shares).

## 5. Thesis check

**Thesis: Slightly weakened but not broken.** The consumer spending environment has been more persistently difficult than the Q1 recovery suggested. SSS declines of 2-3% across geographies signal real organic pressure, and the FX impact on US-market contributions is ongoing as long as the C$ remains weak relative to its prior levels. The corporate-owned segment at -15% YoY is worrying but management is taking the right action — closing 68 underperformers reduces the drag.

The FCF thesis is intact: $32.2M in FCF this quarter, $43.0M in operating CF. The franchise-heavy model generates royalties regardless of same-store headwinds, and the dividend is comfortably covered. Debt is coming down steadily. The key question is whether SSS trends improve in Q3/Q4 (management's comments suggest Q3 will show acceleration in development activity, which is leading indicator of franchisee confidence).

New risk: The 68 corporate store closures will create lumpy impairment charges ($7.5M this quarter; more to come over the next 9 months) that obscure underlying earnings. Also, if SSS declines deepen or spread to the franchisee base, royalty revenue could also begin to soften.

Valuation context: At ~C$55/share [market price not in press release, mark [EST]], with normalized adj EBITDA ~$240M annualized [EST], EV/EBITDA is roughly 10x [EST] — in line with mid-tier QSR/franchise peers but below premium multiples, justified given the current earnings pressure. P/E on depressed GAAP earnings is not meaningful this quarter.

## 6. Watch items for next quarter

1. **SSS recovery**: Did June's Canadian positive trend sustain into July/August? North American SSS turning positive would be the clearest thesis confirmation — or -2%+ continuation would deepen concern.
2. **Corporate store closures**: How many of the 68 underperforming stores close in Q3? Monitor for impairment charges and the net effect on revenue vs. EBITDA margin.
3. **Net debt paydown pace**: On track to reduce below $500M by year-end? With $15.7M repaid in Q2 and FCF ~$30-35M/quarter, $450-480M net debt by Q4 FY2026 is plausible [EST] — watch whether free cash flow holds or whether closures absorb it.
