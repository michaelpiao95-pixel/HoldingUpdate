# MTY Food Group Inc. (MTY) — Earnings Memo
Period: Q1 FY2026 (qtr ended 2026-02-28) | Report Date: 2026-04-10 | Prepared: 2026-07-09
All figures in C$ unless noted.

## 1. Snapshot
- Revenue: $267.8M (YoY -6.0%; QoQ not pulled — see note) — source: MTY Q1 FY2026 release, 2026-04-10
- EPS: GAAP diluted $1.62 vs $0.07 prior-year Q1 — but the jump is largely a base effect (Q1 2025 was hit by impairment charges), not underlying growth. Adjusted EBITDA $59.8M (~22% margin, +2% YoY) is the cleaner read.
- One-line takeaway: Revenue down 6% and same-store sales -2.5% on weak consumer sentiment; profitability held up (EBITDA +2%, strong cash flow), dividend maintained at $0.37. A soft-but-stable quarter, not a thesis-changer.

## 2. What happened this quarter
Revenue fell 6.0% YoY to $267.8M, driven mainly by lower corporate/company-owned store revenue in the US; the franchise-heavy, asset-light model cushioned the profit line. System sales were ~$1.3B. Same-store sales declined 2.5% overall, with a clear geographic split: Canada was resilient at -0.8% while the US dragged at -3.6% (International -1.3%). Management attributed the softness to depressed consumer sentiment, with early signs of improvement noted in March.

Despite the top-line pressure, segment profit / adjusted EBITDA rose 2% to $59.8M (~22% margin) — the point of MTY's model: royalty streams hold up even when franchisee traffic softens. Reported net income of $36.9M ($1.62/sh) looks explosive versus $1.7M ($0.07) a year ago, but that comparison is distorted by prior-year impairment charges; treat it as "profit normalized off a depressed base," not 20x growth.

Unit count contracted: 52 openings against 90 closings (net -38, plus an 8-store TCBY reduction), ending at 7,034 locations. Net closures continue to be the structural watch-item for MTY — management is guiding to store count being a "bright spot" in 2026, so this is a claim to hold them to.

## 3. Guidance & management commentary
MTY does not give formal numeric guidance. CEO Eric Lefebvre struck a measured, steady tone: emphasizing the "asset-light, well diversified model" and its "strong cash flow profile despite persistent macro economic headwinds," and flagging store locations as an expected "bright spot for 2026, continuing the trend from the second half of 2025." Read: management is leaning on the cash-flow/dividend story while acknowledging demand is soft and betting on unit growth turning. No M&A, restructuring, or leadership changes flagged this quarter (MTY is normally acquisitive, so the absence of deal news is itself notable).

## 4. Balance sheet & capital allocation
- Long-term debt $605.1M against $56.0M cash → net debt ~$549M. (The debt is drawn on the revolver: C$250.0M + US$262.0M ≈ C$605M, i.e. these are the same borrowing, not additive.) Leverage not disclosed as a clean ratio in the release [not disclosed]; against ~$60M quarterly EBITDA (~$240M annualized run-rate) that implies roughly [EST] ~2.3x net debt/EBITDA — manageable but not trivial, and worth confirming from the MD&A.
- Cash generation solid: operating cash flow $40.9M (down from $64.6M PY on working-capital timing); free cash flow net of lease payments $29.0M — comfortably covers the dividend.
- Dividend maintained at $0.37/quarter (raised ~12% at the Q4/FY2025 report in Feb 2026). No buyback detail pulled this quarter.

## 5. Thesis check
- Does not change the thesis: MTY remains a cash-generative, asset-light franchisor riding out a weak-consumer patch. Profitability and dividend coverage held despite negative SSS — the model worked as intended.
- New/continuing risk: net unit closures (-38 this quarter) and persistent negative same-store sales, especially in the US (-3.6%). If closures don't reverse as management projects, the royalty base erodes.
- Valuation: not sourced. [EST] With the stock on a "Hold" consensus and results in line-to-soft, no re-rating catalyst this quarter.

## 6. Watch items for next quarter (Q2 FY2026, expected ~mid-July 2026)
1. Same-store sales trend — does the "improvement since March" show up, and does the US gap to Canada narrow?
2. Net unit growth — does the promised 2026 "bright spot" materialize (openings ≥ closings)?
3. Free cash flow and net debt/leverage — dividend coverage and any resumption of M&A or buybacks.
