# Hemisphere Energy Corporation (HME) — Earnings Memo
Period: Q1 2026 (qtr ended 2026-03-31) | Report Date: 2026-05-13 | Prepared: 2026-07-09
All figures in C$ unless noted. Listing: TSXV.

## 1. Snapshot
- Revenue: $25.4M (YoY -7% vs $27.3M; QoQ not pulled) — source: HME Q1 2026 release, 2026-05-13
- EPS/earnings: net income not captured in extract [not disclosed here]; adjusted funds flow (AFF) $12.7M ($0.13/sh), free funds flow $9.6M ($0.10/sh) — the relevant profitability read for an E&P
- One-line takeaway: Stable production (3,811 boe/d) with revenue down modestly on weaker oil prices; strong netback ($48.60/boe), debt-free balance sheet, and heavy shareholder returns (base + special dividends + buyback). Guidance maintained.

## 2. What happened this quarter
Production was essentially flat YoY at 3,811 boe/d (99% heavy oil) vs 3,833, confirming the polymer-flood assets (Atlee Buffalo) are holding volumes as designed. Revenue slipped ~7% to $25.4M — a price story, not a volume story, as heavy-oil realizations were lower YoY. The operating netback of $48.60/boe remained robust for a heavy-oil producer, generating $16.7M of operating netback and $12.7M of adjusted funds flow.

Capital spending was light at $3.1M against $12.7M of AFF, so the quarter threw off $9.6M of free funds flow ($0.10/sh) — the core of the Hemisphere story: low-decline, low-capital, high-netback production that converts to distributable cash. Operationally, the Marsden pilot showed improved injection rates after December downhole modifications, and Atlee Buffalo floods were stable — no negative operational surprises.

## 3. Guidance & management commentary
Management reiterated 2026 guidance of ~3,900 boe/d production. At US$80/bbl WTI, they model ~$57M AFF ($0.60/sh), ~$12M capex, and ~$45M free funds flow ($0.47/sh); guidance is framed across three price scenarios and explicitly supports the $0.10/yr base dividend with no bank debt. Tone is confident and returns-focused — the messaging centers on funding both the base dividend and special dividends out of free cash while keeping the balance sheet debt-free. No strategic shifts (M&A, leadership) flagged.

## 4. Balance sheet & capital allocation
- Working capital surplus $13.9M, zero bank debt — a fortress balance sheet for a micro-cap E&P.
- Shareholder returns were the headline: $0.025/share base quarterly dividend (payable 2026-06-26) plus $0.03/share special dividends in April and May — $0.11/share total in H1 2026. Also repurchased 457,600 shares for $1.0M (avg $2.13) under the NCIB.
- Returns are comfortably covered by free funds flow at current strip; special dividends are the variable lever if prices stay firm.

## 5. Thesis check
- Supports the thesis: the low-decline, debt-free, high-netback, return-of-capital model is working exactly as intended — flat production, strong netback, and cash returned via base + special dividends and buybacks.
- Risk: heavy-oil price and differential exposure (revenue -7% YoY was entirely price-driven). At lower WTI, special dividends and buybacks would be first to be trimmed; the $0.10 base looks safe within guidance. Single-basin/small-scale concentration remains the structural risk.
- Valuation: not sourced. [EST] At ~$0.60/sh AFF guidance ($80 WTI) and ~$2.13 recent buyback price, the stock trades at a low cash-flow multiple typical of micro-cap heavy-oil names; return depends heavily on sustained oil prices.

## 6. Watch items for next quarter (Q2 2026)
1. Heavy-oil price realizations / differentials — the main swing factor on AFF and special dividends.
2. Whether special dividends continue at the H1 pace (a direct read on management's price confidence).
3. Marsden pilot progression and any production uplift toward/above the 3,900 boe/d guide.
