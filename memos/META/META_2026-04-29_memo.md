# Meta Platforms Inc. (META) — Earnings Memo
Period: Q1 FY2026 | Report Date: 2026-04-29 | Prepared: 2026-07-09

## 1. Snapshot
- Revenue: $56.31B (YoY +33%, QoQ -6.0% off the seasonally-high Q4) — source: Q1 2026 press release, 2026-04-29
- EPS: GAAP diluted $10.44 (no separate non-GAAP EPS reported) — vs. prior quarter (Q4 2025) $8.88, +18% QoQ; vs. Q1 2025 $6.43, +62% YoY
- One-line takeaway: Strong beat on revenue and EPS; guidance effectively raised (Q2 revenue midpoint ~$59.5B implies continued growth), but 2026 capex guidance ($125–145B) remains the swing factor.

## 2. What happened this quarter
Revenue grew 33% YoY to $56.31B, an acceleration driven almost entirely by Family of Apps ($55.9B, +33% YoY) as AI-driven ad ranking and targeting continued to lift monetization. Operating income rose to $22.87B at a 41% margin (flat vs. Q1 2025's 41%), so the growth dropped through at a stable margin despite the ongoing AI/infrastructure ramp. The QoQ revenue dip (-6%) is normal Q4→Q1 ad seasonality, not a demand signal.

Reality Labs remains a structural drag: $402M revenue (roughly flat/slightly down YoY) against a $4.03B operating loss. The loss was marginally narrower YoY but the segment is still burning ~$16B/yr annualized.

Diluted EPS of $10.44 came in well above the ~$8.88 prior quarter. Notably, net income ($26.77B) exceeded operating income ($22.87B) by ~$3.9B, pointing to meaningful non-operating income (interest/investment gains) and/or a discrete tax benefit this quarter — the precise driver is not captured in the press-release extract and is flagged [UNVERIFIED]. Underlying operating results, not below-the-line items, are the more reliable read on the quarter.

## 3. Guidance & management commentary
Management guided Q2 2026 revenue to $58–61B (midpoint ~$59.5B), consistent with continued double-digit YoY growth. Full-year 2026 total expenses guided to $162–169B and capex to $125–145B — an enormous AI-infrastructure build. Zuckerberg framed the quarter around "strong momentum across our apps and the release of our first model from Meta Superintelligence Labs," signaling that AI capex is a deliberate, front-loaded investment. Tone is confident/aggressive on spend. The key tension for holders: revenue is comfortably funding the build today, but the capex range is wide and rising, and the market will judge ROI on that spend.

## 4. Balance sheet & capital allocation
- Cash, equivalents & marketable securities: $81.18B; long-term debt $58.75B → modest net cash position, comfortable given FCF generation.
- Free cash flow: $12.39B in the quarter (compressed vs. history by the capex ramp).
- Capital returns: $0 buybacks this quarter (a notable pause vs. Meta's prior heavy repurchase cadence) and $1.35B dividends. The buyback pause is consistent with prioritizing the capex build — worth watching.
- No covenant/liquidity concerns.

## 5. Thesis check
- Supports the thesis: core advertising engine is accelerating (+33%) at a stable 41% margin, and the balance sheet easily absorbs the AI build.
- New/rising risk this quarter: capex guidance ($125–145B) and the $0 buyback quarter — free cash flow is being redirected to infrastructure, so near-term FCF and returns are compressed. Reality Labs loss is unchanged as a drag.
- Valuation: not sourced here. [EST] On ~$40+ annualized GAAP EPS run-rate, valuation depends heavily on how the market capitalizes the AI capex — deliberately left unquantified rather than guessed.

## 6. Watch items for next quarter (Q2 2026, reports ~2026-07-29)
1. Capex trajectory — does full-year guidance move again, and does FCF keep compressing?
2. Resumption (or continued pause) of share buybacks as a tell on capital-allocation priorities.
3. Reality Labs loss trend and any AI-product monetization (Superintelligence Labs models) beginning to show in the numbers.
