# Forvia SE (FRVIA) — Earnings Memo
Period: Q1 FY2026 (sales update) | Report Date: 2026-04-24 | Prepared: 2026-07-13
Figures in €. Listing: Euronext Paris. Note: Forvia reports full P&L half-yearly; Q1 is a revenue-only sales update.

## 1. Snapshot
- Revenue: €5,135M (YoY -2.2% organic; -6.5% reported incl. -4.3% FX) — source: Forvia Q1 2026 sales release, 2026-04-24
- Earnings: no P&L in a Q1 sales update; FY2026 guidance reaffirmed (sales €20–21B, operating margin 6–6.5%)
- One-line takeaway: Sales down organically in a soft auto-production market, but Forvia outperformed global production by ~120 bps and reaffirmed full-year guidance; the story remains margins + deleveraging.

## 2. What happened this quarter
Q1 revenue was €5,135M, down 2.2% organically, with a further 4.3% (€235M) hit from currencies (USD, CNY, JPY) for a reported decline of ~6.5%. The organic decline tracked a weak global light-vehicle production backdrop, but Forvia outperformed underlying auto production by ~120 bps, helped by a favorable geographic mix (~80 bps). Segment performance was highly uneven: Electronics grew 8.2% organically (radar and energy-management technologies — the structural-growth, higher-tech part of the portfolio), while Seating fell 11.1% and Lighting declined 7.3%. The mix shift toward Electronics is strategically favorable even as legacy segments drag.

As a sales-only update, there's no margin or cash data this quarter — the investment questions (margin progression, debt reduction) get answered at the H1 report.

## 3. Guidance & management commentary
Management reaffirmed full-year 2026 guidance: sales of €20–21B and operating margin of 6–6.5%. The consistent strategic message is deleveraging — Forvia carries elevated debt from the Hella acquisition, and debt reduction is the central equity catalyst. Tone: steady/constructive despite the soft market, leaning on the guidance reaffirmation and production outperformance.

## 5. Thesis check
- Neutral-to-supportive: for a deleveraging/turnaround auto-supplier thesis, reaffirmed guidance + ~120 bps production outperformance + Electronics growth are reassuring, but there's no margin/debt data to confirm the deleveraging trajectory this quarter.
- Risks: cyclical auto-production weakness, FX headwinds, high leverage (Hella-related), and pricing pressure in legacy segments (Seating/Lighting).
- Valuation: not sourced. [EST] Classic levered auto-supplier — equity value is highly sensitive to margin delivery (6–6.5%) and net-debt reduction; not quantified without a quote.
