# Wells Fargo & Company (WFC) — Earnings Memo
Period: Q1 FY2026 | Report Date: 2026-04-14 | Prepared: 2026-07-09

## 1. Snapshot
- Revenue: $21,446M (YoY +6.4% vs $20,149M; QoQ +0.7% vs Q4 2025 $21,292M) — source: WFC Q1 2026 release (primary PDF), 2026-04-14
- EPS: GAAP diluted $1.60 (+15% YoY vs $1.39; -1.2% QoQ vs $1.62) — includes a $135M / $0.04 discrete tax benefit this quarter; modest beat vs ~$1.58 consensus
- One-line takeaway: Broad-based ~6% revenue growth with EPS +15% YoY; NII up but NIM compressed 20 bps YoY, and $0.04 of the EPS was a one-time tax benefit. Heavy buyback continues.

## 2. What happened this quarter
Net income was $5,253M (+7% YoY vs $4,894M; -2% QoQ vs $5,361M) on revenue of $21,446M (+6.4% YoY). Growth was broad-based — every operating segment grew revenue YoY. Net interest income rose 5% YoY to $12,096M, but NIM fell 20 bps YoY to 2.47% (and down from 2.60% in Q4 2025), so NII growth is being carried by volume (average loans +10% YoY to $996.0B, average deposits +6% to $1,415.0B) rather than spread. Noninterest income rose 8% YoY — notable because the prior-year quarter already included a $263M gain on the sale of the commercial-mortgage servicing business, so the fee line grew despite a tougher comparison.

Reported EPS of $1.60 included a $135M ($0.04/share) discrete tax benefit from resolution of prior-period matters — i.e., ~$1.56 of "clean" EPS. Credit stayed benign: provision for credit losses of $1,135M, with total net loan charge-offs stable at 45 bps (commercial NCOs 24 bps, up slightly from 22 bps). Efficiency ratio was 67% (vs 69% a year ago, an improvement, though up from 64% in Q4). Returns were healthy: ROE 12.2%, ROTCE 14.5%.

QoQ, results were essentially flat (revenue +0.7%, EPS -$0.02). The Q4 2025 comparison is noisy — that quarter's GAAP EPS ($1.62) absorbed a $612M severance charge (ex-item ~$1.76 [secondary]) — so on an underlying basis Q1 stepped down sequentially, and stripping out Q1's $0.04 tax benefit widens that gap.

## 3. Guidance & management commentary
CEO Charlie Scharf's tone was confident and growth-oriented. Verbatim: *"We saw continued positive impacts from the investments we have been making with diluted earnings per share increasing 15%, revenue increasing 6%, loans increasing 11%, and deposits increasing 7% compared to a year ago... Credit performance remained strong with net loan charge-offs stable at 45 basis points. We returned $4 billion to shareholders through common stock repurchases while continuing to operate with significant excess capital."* The strategic backdrop remains the 2025 removal of the Fed asset cap, freeing WFC to grow the balance sheet — this quarter's +11% period-end loan growth is the early evidence. (FY2026 NII outlook of ~$50B and noninterest expense ~$55.7B are from the analyst deck [secondary], not restated in the release text I extracted.) No new restructuring or leadership changes flagged.

## 4. Balance sheet & capital allocation
- CET1 ratio 10.3% — comfortably above regulatory minimums, leaving room for continued returns.
- Capital return was aggressive: $4.0B of common stock repurchased (46.3M shares) plus a $0.45/share common dividend. Buybacks remain the primary lever for EPS support given only mid-single-digit revenue growth.
- Deposits (+6–7%) and loans (+10–11%) both growing; no liquidity or covenant flags.

## 5. Thesis check
- Supports/does not change the thesis: the core WFC story — post-asset-cap balance-sheet growth plus heavy buybacks driving EPS — is intact and visible in the +10% loan growth and $4.0B repurchase.
- New/watch risk: NIM compression (-20 bps YoY, and -13 bps QoQ to 2.47%) means NII growth is volume-dependent; if loan growth slows or deposit costs rise, the ~$50B NII target is at risk. Also note ~$0.04 of Q1 EPS was a non-recurring tax benefit, so the underlying EPS beat vs consensus was thinner than the headline.
- Valuation: not sourced here. [EST] At ROTCE ~14.5% and CET1 10.3%, the capital-return runway is the main value driver; deliberately not quantifying P/E or P/TBV without a sourced figure.

## 6. Watch items for next quarter (Q2 2026, reports 2026-07-14)
1. NIM trajectory and whether FY2026 NII guidance (~$50B) is reaffirmed, raised, or trimmed.
2. Buyback pace — does WFC sustain ~$4B/quarter, and what does CET1 do?
3. Credit normalization — provision and net charge-off rate direction, especially commercial.
