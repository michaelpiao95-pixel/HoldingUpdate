# eBay Inc. (EBAY) — Earnings Memo
Period: Q1 FY2026 | Report Date: 2026-04-29 | Prepared: 2026-07-11

## 1. Snapshot
- Revenue: $3,089M (YoY +19% reported, +17% FX-neutral) — source: eBay Q1 2026 release, 2026-04-29
- EPS: GAAP diluted $1.12 (+7% YoY) | Non-GAAP $1.66 (+21% YoY; beat ~$1.58) — GAAP net income (cont. ops) $512M; Non-GAAP $760M
- One-line takeaway: Strong top-line beat (GMV +18%, revenue +19%) led by collectibles/Goldin, but operating margins compressed meaningfully — the tension of the quarter.

## 2. What happened this quarter
Growth accelerated sharply: GMV rose 18% to $22.2B and net revenues jumped 19% to $3,089M — well above eBay's recent low-single-digit trajectory. The driver was Focus Categories, C2C/recommerce, and especially collectibles: Collectibles GMV +24% (Pokémon 30th anniversary and record trading-card demand), and Goldin (an eBay company) posted an all-time quarterly GMV record. Advertising remained a steady monetization layer at $555M (2.6% of GMV).

The catch was margins. GAAP operating margin fell to 19.8% (from 23.6% a year ago) and non-GAAP to 29.4% (from ~32%). So while GMV/revenue and non-GAAP EPS (+21%) were strong, the growth came at a lower incremental margin — likely mix (lower-margin categories/Goldin) and investment. GAAP EPS grew only 7% ($1.12) versus non-GAAP +21% ($1.66), a gap that reflects the margin pressure and non-GAAP add-backs.

## 3. Guidance & management commentary
Management framed the quarter as accelerating GMV growth and performance ahead of expectations, crediting Focus Categories and recommerce. Tone: confident on growth. (Specific Q2/FY2026 numeric guidance not captured in the extracted release text [not captured this run].) The strategic read: eBay is leaning into high-velocity verticals (trading cards/collectibles via Goldin) to reignite GMV — the open question is whether that mix permanently resets margins lower.

## 4. Balance sheet & capital allocation
- Capital returned: $639M — $500M buybacks (~6M shares) + $139M dividends; ~$2.3B buyback authorization remaining.
- Buybacks continue to shrink the share count and support EPS.
- No liquidity flags noted in the release.

## 5. Thesis check
- Mixed but net-supportive: the long-standing knock on eBay was stagnant GMV; +18% GMV growth directly addresses that. But the ~380 bps GAAP operating-margin decline is a new wrinkle — growth is being bought at lower margins.
- New risk this quarter: margin compression and reliance on collectibles/trading-card cyclicality (a category that can cool as fast as it heated).
- Valuation: not sourced. [EST] Re-rating depends on whether the market credits the GMV acceleration as durable vs. a collectibles-driven spike; not quantified without a quote.

## 6. Watch items for next quarter (Q2 2026, reports ~late-July 2026)
1. GMV growth durability — does the collectibles/Goldin surge sustain or fade?
2. Operating margin trajectory — does the ~380 bps GAAP compression stabilize or continue?
3. Buyback pace against the ~$2.3B remaining authorization.
