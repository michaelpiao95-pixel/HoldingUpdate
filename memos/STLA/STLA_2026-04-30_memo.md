# Stellantis N.V. (STLA) — Earnings Memo
Period: Q1 FY2026 (qtr ended 2026-03-31) | Report Date: 2026-04-30 | Prepared: 2026-07-11
Figures in €. Note: Stellantis reports full P&L half-yearly; Q1 is a revenues/shipments + AOI + net-profit summary.

## 1. Snapshot
- Revenue: €38.1B (YoY +6%) — source: Stellantis Q1 2026 financial results, 2026-04-30
- Earnings: net profit €0.4B (€377M); adjusted operating income €1.0B at a 2.5% AOI margin
- One-line takeaway: Early turnaround evidence — shipments +12%, revenue +6%, back to (thin) profitability after a weak 2025, but industrial free cash flow is still deeply negative (−€1.9B) and margins are only low-single-digit.

## 2. What happened this quarter
After a difficult 2025, Q1 2026 shows the beginnings of a recovery. Consolidated shipments rose 12% YoY to 1,361k units and net revenues grew 6% to €38.1B, with the improvement led by North America (sales +6%, U.S. Ram strength, market share up 80 bps to 7.9%) and Enlarged Europe (sales +5%, +8% including Leapmotor; EU30 share 17.5%). The company returned to profitability with net profit of €0.4B and adjusted operating income of €1.0B — but the AOI margin of just 2.5% underscores that this is an early-stage, low-profitability recovery, not a return to Stellantis's former double-digit margins.

The clear negative remains cash: industrial free cash flow was −€1.9B. Management frames this as a ~37% improvement YoY, and guides to positive industrial FCF only in 2027 — so 2026 is a transition year where volumes and profit recover ahead of cash.

## 3. Guidance & management commentary
Full-year 2026 guidance: net revenues up mid-single-digit %, AOI margin low-single-digit %, and industrial free cash flow improved YoY (with positive FCF expected in 2027). CEO Antonio Filosa: "The first three months of 2026 reflect early results of our actions to return Stellantis to sustainable, profitable growth." Tone: cautiously constructive — management is explicitly positioning 2026 as a rebuild year. The €5B hybrid-note issuance (reported around the quarter) reinforces liquidity/flexibility during the transition.

## 4. Balance sheet & capital allocation
- Industrial available liquidity €44.1B (~28% of trailing-12-month revenues) — strong liquidity cushion through the turnaround.
- Industrial free cash flow −€1.9B (improving); positive not expected until 2027.
- Capital returns (dividend/buyback) not detailed in the Q1 summary [not disclosed] — Stellantis's dividend policy is typically set with full-year results.

## 5. Thesis check
- Tentatively supportive: for a deep-value/turnaround holding, the +12% shipments, +6% revenue, and return to profit are the direction bulls needed. But it's early and thin — 2.5% AOI margin and −€1.9B FCF mean the thesis is unproven.
- Risk: margins remain fragile; tariffs/trade (North America), EV transition costs, and pricing are all swing factors. Cash burn continues through 2026.
- Valuation: not sourced. [EST] Classic low-multiple turnaround — the re-rating case depends on margin recovery toward mid-single-digits and FCF turning positive in 2027; not quantified without a quote.

## 6. Watch items for next quarter / H1 2026 report
1. AOI margin trajectory — any move off the 2.5% low toward the guided low-single-digit full-year.
2. Industrial free cash flow — pace of improvement toward the 2027 positive-FCF target.
3. North America momentum (Ram/share gains) and Europe pricing; tariff/trade impacts.
