# Movado Group Inc. (MOV) — Earnings Memo
Period: Q1 FY2027 (qtr ended 2026-04-30) | Report Date: 2026-05-27 | Prepared: 2026-07-13

## 1. Snapshot
- Revenue: $142.4M net sales (YoY +8.1% reported, +4.5% constant-dollar) — source: Movado Q1 FY2027 release, 2026-05-27
- EPS: GAAP diluted $0.30 (vs $0.06 PY) | Adjusted $0.32 (vs $0.08) — operating income $7.0M (adjusted $7.5M)
- One-line takeaway: Strong rebound quarter — sales +8%, gross margin up 320 bps to 57.3%, EPS up ~5x off a weak base; debt-free with $225M cash and a raised dividend.

## 2. What happened this quarter
Movado delivered a clear recovery. Net sales grew 8.1% (4.5% constant-dollar) to $142.4M, with increases across owned and licensed brands and Movado Company Stores — a sign that demand and sell-through improved versus a soft prior year. Profitability improved even faster: gross margin expanded 320 bps to 57.3%, driving operating income to $7.0M and diluted EPS to $0.30 (from just $0.06 a year ago). The magnitude of the EPS jump reflects both real operating improvement and an easy comparison against a depressed Q1 FY2026.

The balance sheet remains a defining strength: $225.3M cash and no debt, giving Movado unusual resilience for a discretionary consumer name and ample capacity to fund dividends, buybacks, or brand investment.

## 3. Guidance & management commentary
Management signaled confidence via capital return: the Board raised the quarterly dividend $0.05 to $0.40/share. (Full FY2027 numeric guidance not captured this run [not captured].) Tone read: constructive — margin recovery and dividend increase point to management comfort with the demand trajectory, though watch/discretionary-consumer categories remain exposed to sentiment and tariffs. Strategic emphasis remains premium/licensed brand positioning and direct retail (Company Stores).

## 4. Balance sheet & capital allocation
- $225.3M cash, no debt — a fortress balance sheet worth a meaningful share of the market cap.
- Dividend raised to $0.40/quarter (+$0.05); Movado also historically runs a buyback.
- No liquidity or covenant concerns; the cash pile is both downside protection and optionality.

## 5. Thesis check
- Supports the thesis: for a deep-value, cash-rich consumer name, the +8% sales, 57.3% gross margin, and 5x EPS growth are exactly the recovery bulls wanted, and the dividend hike reinforces it.
- Risk: watches are discretionary and FX/tariff-exposed; the EPS growth is flattered by an easy comp, so the run-rate matters more than the YoY %. Licensed-brand renewals and wholesale channel health to watch.
- Valuation: not sourced. [EST] A large fraction of market cap is net cash; ex-cash, the operating business trades cheaply — re-rating hinges on sustained margin/sales recovery.

## 6. Watch items for next quarter (Q2 FY2027)
1. Constant-dollar sales growth (the cleaner read vs. the +8.1% reported) and whether momentum holds off the easy comp.
2. Gross margin sustainability around 57%+.
3. Capital deployment of the $225M cash — buybacks/dividends vs. brand investment; tariff impacts.
