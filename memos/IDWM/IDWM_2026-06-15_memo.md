## 2. What happened this quarter
IDW Media is essentially IDW Publishing (comics and graphic novels — home to licensed titles and original IP). Q2 publishing revenue rose 16% to $5.5M, and the net loss narrowed sharply to $(144)K from $(533)K a year ago. Over the first half of fiscal 2026 (six months to April 30), total revenue exceeded $11M (+~16%) and the net loss was cut to just $(3)K — essentially breakeven, a dramatic improvement for a company that has been loss-making. Comics/graphic-novel revenue rose 14%, and management credited recovery from the Diamond (distributor) bankruptcy, stronger "IDW Dark" horror-imprint sales, and backlist growth. Operating losses fell sharply.

This is a small-cap turnaround showing real operational progress — top-line growth plus a hard swing toward breakeven.

## 3. Guidance & management commentary
No formal guidance captured. The strategic narrative is stabilization and growth of the publishing business after industry disruption (Diamond's bankruptcy reshaped comics distribution), leaning on original IP (IDW Dark), licensed titles, and backlist. Tone read: cautiously improving. The key questions: can IDW convert near-breakeven into sustained profitability, and can it monetize IP beyond publishing (media/licensing)?
