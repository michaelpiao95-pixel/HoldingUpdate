## 2. What happened this quarter
Glacier is mid-transformation from a legacy print/community-media business into a data and digital-media company (environmental, agricultural, and energy data/markets). Q1 revenue fell 8.1% to $29.8M, with $1.3M of the $2.6M decline coming directly from legacy print community media — i.e., the managed run-off of the declining business. The important offset: consolidated EBITDA swung to +$1.0M from a $(1.2)M loss a year earlier — a $2.3M YoY improvement — driven by direct and G&A expenses falling $4.9M through cost containment and rationalization, partly offset by investment in strategic growth (data/digital) areas.

So the quarter fits the transformation playbook: shrink/harvest legacy print, cut costs hard, and reinvest in higher-value data assets — with the payoff showing first in EBITDA rather than revenue.

## 3. Guidance & management commentary
No formal guidance captured. The strategic narrative is the pivot to data/digital while rationalizing legacy operations. Tone read: disciplined/cost-focused with selective growth investment. The key judgment for holders is whether the growing data/digital segments can eventually outgrow the declining print drag and return the whole company to revenue growth — this quarter shows cost control is working, but not yet top-line stabilization.
