# Allied Gold Corporation (AAUC) — Earnings Memo
Period: Q1 FY2026 | Report Date: 2026-05-14 | Prepared: 2026-07-13

## 1. Snapshot
- Production: 96,016 oz gold (YoY +14%); AISC $2,264/oz — source: Allied Gold Q1 2026 release, 2026-05-14
- EPS: GAAP $(0.47) (net loss $58.3M) | Adjusted $0.39 (adjusted earnings $48.6M) — the GAAP loss is driven by non-cash/one-time items
- One-line takeaway: Operationally strong (production +14%, healthy operating cash flow at high gold prices), but a GAAP net loss and — the dominant factor — a pending acquisition by Zijin Gold that now frames the entire investment.

## 2. What happened this quarter
Operationally this was a good quarter: gold production rose 14% YoY to 96,016 oz, and cash generation was strong — operating cash flow before tax and working capital of $162.7M, and $57.3M of net cash from operating activities — helped by average realized gold prices around $4,775/oz. AISC of $2,264/oz was in line, with ~$80/oz of the cost being higher royalties tied to the elevated gold price (a "good problem").

The headline GAAP net loss of $58.3M ($(0.47)/sh) contrasts sharply with adjusted earnings of $48.6M ($0.39/sh) — a ~$107M gap that reflects non-cash and/or one-time items (FX, derivatives, deferred tax, or deal-related costs; the precise composition wasn't captured this run [UNVERIFIED]). Adjusted earnings are the better read on underlying profitability, which was solidly positive.

## 3. Guidance & management commentary
The defining item is corporate, not operational: Allied Gold is progressing a transaction with Zijin Gold (Zijin acquiring Allied). This effectively converts the thesis from "operating gold producer" to "deal/arbitrage situation" — the stock will trade primarily on deal terms, closing probability, and timing/regulatory approvals rather than quarterly results. Management framed the quarter around advancing its growth strategy and progressing the Zijin transaction. Tone: constructive on operations, deal-focused.
