# Zimtu Capital Corp. (ZC) — Earnings Memo
Period: FY2025 (year ended 2025-11-30) | Report Date: 2026-03-26 (MD&A effective date; posted Apr 2026) | Prepared: 2026-07-09
All figures in C$. Listing: TSXV (also Frankfurt: ZCT1).

## 1. Snapshot
- Revenue/income: not a conventional revenue company — FY2025 income before other items $4.33M (FY2024 $2.15M); reported net income $19.28M (FY2024 $5.90M) — source: FY2025 MD&A
- EPS: ≈$1.43/share basic (approximate — table layout garbled in extraction) — no adjusted figure; no dividend
- One-line takeaway: A blowout year on paper (net income +227% to $19.3M), but ~80% of it is unrealized mark-to-market gains on the investment portfolio (led by Apex Critical Metals). Book value/NAV, not "earnings," is the right lens here.

## 2. What happened this year
Zimtu is an investment holding company / prospect generator: it takes early-stage equity and royalty stakes in junior resource companies and books their fair-value changes through income. So FY2025 net income of $19.28M (vs $5.90M in FY2024) is dominated by non-cash, unrealized gains, not operations. The MD&A says so explicitly: the swing in income "is due to the increase in the unrealized gains and losses of investments held by the Company due to changes in market values."

The operating businesses were modest but positive: administration fee income $1.83M, ZimtuADVANTAGE corporate-development services $1.09M, property-sale income $1.41M (vs a $0.58M loss in 2024), and $0.14M gain on sale of investments. Income before other items (a cleaner proxy for the operating engine) was $4.33M, up from $2.15M — real improvement, but an order of magnitude smaller than the headline net income. Cash from operations was just $0.83M, underscoring that the profit is on paper.

The value creation is concentrated: the Apex Critical Metals stake alone was marked at $14.53M at year-end, up from $4.35M — i.e., a single holding drove the bulk of the gain. That is both the bull and bear case: enormous torque to one or two junior names.

## 3. Guidance & management commentary
No formal guidance (typical for this structure). Strategically, FY2025/early-2026 activity shows the model in motion: monetizing properties (Crystal Lake sold to Discovery, Copperline to Star Copper — both for cash + shares + NSR royalties), a 5:1 share consolidation (May 2025) that cut shares from 63.4M to 12.7M, a warrant-incentive raise ($2.09M, Nov 2025), and a Jan-2026 option to acquire 50% of the Monument Peak property (Idaho). Tone is opportunistic/deal-driven. One governance note: director Chris Grove resigned (July 2025).

## 4. Balance sheet & capital allocation
- Total assets $39.63M (vs $17.29M) — the balance sheet more than doubled, almost entirely from portfolio appreciation.
- Investments at fair value $34.29M; cash $2.06M (up from $0.21M); working capital $39.06M.
- Long-term liabilities $0.75M; no bank debt flagged — effectively unlevered.
- No dividend and no buyback; capital is recycled into new resource stakes. NAV per share (a better metric than EPS here) is [EST] roughly $39M working capital / 12.68M shares ≈ ~$3.08/sh [EST, calculated] — worth confirming against the audited statements, and note it moves with junior-mining prices daily.

## 5. Thesis check
- Supports the thesis if you own ZC as a levered, discounted proxy on junior critical-metals/resource equities: NAV roughly doubled and the balance sheet is clean and unlevered.
- Key risk (elevated this year): concentration and mark-to-market volatility. ~$14.5M of the $34.3M portfolio is one name (Apex); a resource-sector drawdown would reverse much of FY2025's "earnings" just as fast. Reported net income is not cash and not repeatable.
- Valuation: the only meaningful metric is price vs NAV (~$3/sh [EST]); prospect generators typically trade at a discount to NAV. Not sourced to a market quote here.
