# Goodfood Market Corp. (FOOD) — Earnings Memo
Period: Q2 FY2026 (13 wks ended 2026-03-07) | Report Date: 2026-04-21 | Prepared: 2026-07-13
Figures in C$. Listing: TSX. Note: Goodfood's fiscal year ends ~early September.

## 1. Snapshot
- Revenue: net sales ~$23M — source: Goodfood Q2 FY2026 release, 2026-04-21
- Earnings: gross profit ~$7M (gross margin 30.6%); adjusted EBITDA loss ~$1M; adjusted FCF -$2.0M
- One-line takeaway: A weak, disrupted quarter — a temporary CFIA licence suspension plus soft demand pushed Goodfood back to an adjusted-EBITDA loss and negative FCF, reversing Q1's small profit; management is pursuing a "strategic reset" focused on cash.

## 2. What happened this quarter
Q2 was a setback after a stabilizing Q1. Net sales were ~$23M with gross margin of 30.6% — a sharp margin drop from Q1's 42.3% — and the business swung to an adjusted EBITDA loss of ~$1M and adjusted free cash flow of -$2.0M. The primary culprit was operational: a temporary Canadian Food Inspection Agency (CFIA) licence suspension disrupted operations, compounded by a softer demand environment in an already-shrinking meal-kit market. For context, Q1 FY2026 (ended Dec 6, 2025) had net sales of $27.5M (down 21% YoY) but positive adjusted EBITDA of $1M and positive FCF — so Q2's deterioration is both a one-off disruption and a continuation of secular volume decline.

The through-line: Goodfood is a shrinking-revenue turnaround, trying to hold margins and cash while the meal-kit category contracts.

## 3. Guidance & management commentary
Management framed Q2 around a "strategic reset focused on cash generation and core economics" — i.e., prioritizing unit economics, cost control, and cash preservation over growth. Tone: defensive/restructuring. No formal numeric guidance captured. The strategic emphasis on cash and core economics signals management recognizes the category headwind and is managing for survival/profitability rather than expansion.

## 4. Balance sheet & capital allocation
- Adjusted FCF was -$2.0M this quarter — cash burn resumed after Q1's small positive FCF, making liquidity the key thing to monitor.
- Cash balance / debt / convertible-debenture position not captured this run [not captured] — for a micro-cap in decline, liquidity runway is the critical figure; confirm from the release/MD&A.
- No dividend/buyback (cash preservation mode).

## 5. Thesis check
- Weakens: the CFIA disruption plus renewed EBITDA/FCF losses undercut the "stabilizing turnaround" narrative that Q1 had supported.
- Risks: secular meal-kit decline, thin/negative margins, operational/regulatory disruptions (CFIA), and liquidity runway; this is a high-risk micro-cap turnaround.
- Valuation: not sourced. [EST] A cash-burn micro-cap; value hinges entirely on reaching sustained positive FCF before liquidity is exhausted — the "strategic reset" is a survival plan, not a growth story.

## 6. Watch items for next quarter (Q3 FY2026)
1. Recovery from the CFIA licence suspension — does revenue/margin bounce back?
2. Return to positive adjusted EBITDA and free cash flow (the reset's stated goal).
3. Cash/liquidity runway and any financing needs.
