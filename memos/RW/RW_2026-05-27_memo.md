# Renoworks Software Inc. (RW) — Earnings Memo
Period: Q1 FY2026 | Report Date: 2026-05-27 | Prepared: 2026-07-13
Figures in C$. Listing: TSXV.

## 1. Snapshot
- Revenue: $1.84M (YoY -1% vs $1.85M) — source: Renoworks Q1 2026 release, 2026-05-27
- Recurring revenue: $1.19M (+36% YoY); gross margin 80% (up from 75%)
- One-line takeaway: Flat headline revenue masks a healthy mix shift — high-margin recurring licensing +36% offsetting a planned decline in one-time design services; margins expanding as the SaaS transition progresses (though still net-loss-making).

## 2. What happened this quarter
Renoworks (visualization software for building-products manufacturers and contractors) is executing a deliberate shift from lumpy services revenue to recurring software. Total revenue was essentially flat at $1.84M (-1%), but the composition improved markedly: recurring licensing & hosting revenue jumped 36% to $1.19M (now ~65% of revenue), offsetting an expected decline in lower-margin design services. That mix shift lifted gross margin to 80% (from 75%). Revenue by line: licensing/hosting $1.19M, implementation $304K, design services $191K, libraries $148K.

The trade-off: a secondary summary flagged a wider net loss, so the company is still investing ahead of scale (the exact net-loss figure wasn't captured this run [not captured]). The quarter fits the classic small SaaS transition — flat/soft headline revenue, rising recurring mix and gross margin, continued losses while scaling.

## 3. Guidance & management commentary
No formal guidance captured. The strategy is explicit: transition to a higher-margin recurring-revenue software model with AI-driven capabilities across enterprise and contractor solutions. Tone read: focused on recurring-revenue growth and margin. The +36% recurring growth and 80% gross margin are the proof points management is pointing to; the open question is when the recurring base is large enough to cross into sustained profitability.

## 5. Thesis check
- Supports the (SaaS-transition) thesis on quality: +36% recurring revenue and 80% gross margin show the model shift is working, even with flat total revenue.
- Risks: still loss-making with limited scale; dependence on building-products/renovation cyclicality; customer concentration; and cash runway for a micro-cap. Total-revenue growth needs to reaccelerate once the services decline anniversaries.
