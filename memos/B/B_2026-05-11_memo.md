# Barrick Mining Corporation (B) — Earnings Memo
Period: Q1 FY2026 | Report Date: 2026-05-11 | Prepared: 2026-07-13

## 1. Snapshot
- Revenue: $5.22B — source: Barrick Q1 2026 release, 2026-05-11
- EPS: GAAP $0.96 (net earnings $1.60B, +256% YoY) | Adjusted $0.98 (+180% YoY) — vs $0.27/$0.35 a year ago
- One-line takeaway: Big beat on higher gold prices and strong output — production beat guidance, AISC fell 4%, huge FCF, and Barrick added a $3.0B buyback on top of the base dividend.

## 2. What happened this quarter
A gold-price-driven blowout. Realized gold prices were up 66% YoY, and Barrick produced 719,000 oz — above its own 640,000–680,000 oz guidance — thanks to NGM, Veladero, and the Loulo-Gounkoto ramp. That combination sent net earnings to $1.60B ($0.96/sh, +256%) and adjusted earnings to $1.65B ($0.98/sh, +180%). All-in sustaining costs of $1,708/oz actually fell 4% YoY, so margins expanded on both price and cost — the ideal setup for a gold miner. Copper (49,000 t) was a smaller contributor, with C1 costs $2.57/lb and copper AISC $3.67/lb rising YoY.

Cash generation was the headline underneath: $2.55B operating cash flow, $1.97B attributable operating cash flow, and $1.21B attributable free cash flow in a single quarter.

## 3. Guidance & management commentary
Full-year production and cost guidance was maintained, with Q2 2026 production guided to 730,000–770,000 oz (a sequential step-up). Management struck a confident tone on executing guidance and advancing growth projects "on time and on budget." The most important capital-allocation signals: a $0.175/share base quarterly dividend (paid June 15) under a policy targeting 50% of attributable FCF annually (base + year-end top-up), and a newly authorized share buyback of up to $3.0B following strong Q1 FCF. Note the corporate identity shift to "Barrick Mining Corporation" (from Barrick Gold) reflecting the copper strategy.

## 4. Balance sheet & capital allocation
- $1.21B attributable FCF in the quarter funds both dividend and the new buyback comfortably.
- New up-to-$3.0B share repurchase program — a clear signal management views the stock as attractive and FCF as durable at current gold prices.
- Base dividend $0.175/quarter with a performance top-up mechanism tied to FCF.

## 5. Thesis check
- Strongly supports the thesis: this is what owning a low-cost senior gold producer into a rising gold-price environment looks like — production beat, costs down, record-ish FCF, and expanded capital return.
- Risk: earnings are highly levered to the gold price (the +256% was mostly price). A gold pullback would compress the same margins quickly. Copper cost inflation and project execution are secondary watch items.
- Valuation: not sourced. [EST] At ~$0.98 quarterly adjusted EPS run-rate (gold-price-dependent) plus dividend + $3B buyback, the FCF yield looks strong if gold holds; not quantified without a quote.

## 6. Watch items for next quarter (Q2 2026)
1. Gold price and realized price — the dominant earnings driver.
2. Buyback execution pace against the $3.0B authorization, and the year-end dividend top-up trajectory.
3. Production ramp (Loulo-Gounkoto) toward the 730–770k oz Q2 guide; AISC trend.
