# Alibaba Group Holding Limited (BABA) — Earnings Memo
Period: March Quarter FY2026 (qtr ended 2026-03-31; fiscal Q4 FY2026) | Report Date: 2026-05-12 | Prepared: 2026-07-11
Figures in RMB with US$ as reported.

## 1. Snapshot
- Revenue: RMB243,380M (US$35,283M) — YoY +3% — source: Alibaba March Quarter/FY2026 results, 2026-05-12
- EPS: GAAP diluted RMB10.36/ADS (US$1.50) | Non-GAAP diluted RMB0.62/ADS (US$0.09), −95% YoY — GAAP net income attributable RMB25,476M (US$3,693M)
- One-line takeaway: A deliberate "investment mode" quarter — revenue only +3% and adjusted EBITA −84% as Alibaba pours money into AI, cloud, and quick commerce; GAAP net income (+96%) is flattered by investment gains, not operations.

## 2. What happened this quarter
This is a story of two very different lines. The top line was pedestrian (+3% to RMB243.4B), and profitability collapsed on purpose: the company posted a loss from operations of RMB(848)M (vs +RMB28,465M a year ago) and adjusted EBITA fell 84% YoY. Management is spending aggressively — quick-commerce build-out, Qwen AI-app user acquisition, and cloud infrastructure capex — and it shows up directly in margins and cash. Operating cash flow fell 66% to ~RMB9.4B, and free cash flow was an outflow of RMB(17,300)M (US$(2.5)B).

The bright spot is Cloud Intelligence Group: external cloud revenue accelerated to +40% YoY, with AI-related product revenue posting triple-digit growth for the 11th consecutive quarter and now ~30% of cloud external revenue. China e-commerce customer management revenue grew 8% like-for-like. So the strategic pivot — AI + cloud as the growth engine — is producing real cloud acceleration, financed by crushing near-term group profitability.

GAAP net income attributable rose to RMB25,476M and net income +96% YoY — but this was driven by mark-to-market/gains on equity investments, not the operating business (which lost money at the EBITA line). Non-GAAP diluted EPS/ADS of US$0.09 (−95% YoY) is the truer read on operating earnings power this quarter.

## 3. Guidance & management commentary
CEO Eddie Wu framed the quarter around AI/cloud breakthroughs: "Cloud Intelligence Group's external revenue growth accelerated to 40%, with AI-related products accounting for 30% of this revenue," and committed to "continue to invest in AI + Cloud to strengthen our competitive advantages." Tone: aggressive, investment-forward, willing to sacrifice near-term profit for AI/cloud and quick-commerce share. No formal numeric guidance (typical). Strategic thrust: quick commerce + AI are the two big capital sinks; cloud is the intended payoff.

- Free cash flow negative RMB(17.3)B this quarter on the investment ramp — a marked swing from Alibaba's historically strong FCF.

## 5. Thesis check
- Mixed: the cloud/AI acceleration (+40% external) validates the long-term bull case, but the −84% adjusted EBITA and negative FCF show how expensive the pivot is right now. Whether this is value-creating hinges entirely on cloud/AI ROI.
- New/elevated risk: margin and cash-flow deterioration from quick-commerce + AI spend; GAAP net income is being propped by volatile investment gains, masking operating weakness.
- Valuation: not sourced. [EST] The market is being asked to underwrite AI/cloud optionality against visibly negative near-term FCF — a wide range of outcomes; not quantified without a quote.

## 6. Watch items for next quarter (Q1 FY2027, ~Aug 2026)
1. Adjusted EBITA / operating margin — does the investment drag stabilize or deepen?
2. Free cash flow — how negative, and for how long, before cloud/AI monetization offsets it?
3. Cloud external revenue growth (can it hold ~40%?) and quick-commerce losses.
