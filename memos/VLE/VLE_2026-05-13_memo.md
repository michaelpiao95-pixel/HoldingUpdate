# Valeura Energy Inc. (VLE) — Earnings Memo
Period: Q1 FY2026 | Report Date: 2026-05-13 | Prepared: 2026-07-13
Figures in US$. Listing: TSX.

## 1. Snapshot
- Production: 22,326 bbl/d WI before royalties (YoY -6%) — source: Valeura Q1 2026 results, 2026-05-13
- Cash flow: adjusted cash flow from operations US$21.3M (-76% YoY) — depressed by lifting/sales timing, not underlying weakness
- One-line takeaway: Optically weak Q1 due to March liftings deferred into April — but net cash rose to US$261.6M (no debt), and April alone generated US$90.3M revenue (≈ all of Q1). A timing distortion, not a deterioration.

## 2. What happened this quarter
The quarter reads poorly at the headline — production down 6% to 22,326 bbl/d and adjusted cash flow from operations down 76% to US$21.3M — but the cause is almost entirely timing. Valeura sells oil in discrete cargo liftings, and March liftings were deferred into Q2; those barrels were then sold in April at higher oil prices, generating US$90.3M of revenue in April alone (nearly matching the entire Q1 total). So Q1 understates the business, and Q2 will overstate it. Operating costs were in line with guidance (adjusted opex US$25.4/bbl; operating costs US$15.6/bbl), and all four Gulf of Thailand fields (Jasmine, Manora, Nong Yao, Wassana) produced through the quarter.

The most important number is the balance sheet: net cash grew to US$261.6M with no debt — a very large cash position relative to Valeura's size, built from prior strong cash generation.

## 3. Guidance & management commentary
Management framed the quarter as "resilient," emphasizing the lifting-timing explanation and the strong April revenue as evidence the underlying business is healthy. Tone: confident, pointing past the optical Q1 weakness. Strategic focus remains maximizing Gulf of Thailand output and using the large net-cash balance for growth (infill drilling, potential acquisitions/inorganic growth in SE Asia). No debt and rising cash give management wide optionality.

## 4. Balance sheet & capital allocation
- Net cash US$261.6M, zero debt — the standout feature; funds organic growth and potential M&A without financing risk.
- Cash generation is lumpy quarter-to-quarter due to lifting timing — judge on trailing-twelve-months, not single quarters.
- No dividend/buyback detail captured this run [not captured]; capital has been directed at growth and building the cash buffer.

## 5. Thesis check
- Does not change the thesis: the timing distortion aside, Valeura is a debt-free, cash-rich Gulf-of-Thailand producer with strong netbacks — Q1's optics don't reflect a real deterioration, as the April revenue confirms.
- Risks: oil price and lifting-timing volatility (makes quarters noisy); asset concentration in mature Gulf of Thailand fields (decline management); Thailand fiscal/regulatory and abandonment liabilities; capital-deployment risk if M&A is pursued.
- Valuation: not sourced. [EST] With ~US$262M net cash against a small-cap valuation, a large share of value is cash; the operating business is priced cheaply on cash flow if oil holds.

## 6. Watch items for next quarter (Q2 2026)
1. Q2 cash flow — should rebound sharply as deferred March/April liftings are recognized (watch for the timing "catch-up").
2. Production trend across the four fields and any infill-drilling additions.
3. Deployment of the US$262M net cash — acquisitions, drilling, or shareholder returns.
