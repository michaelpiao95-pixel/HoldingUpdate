# Transocean Ltd. (RIG) — Earnings Memo
Period: Q1 FY2026 | Report Date: 2026-05-04 | Prepared: 2026-07-11

## 1. Snapshot
- Revenue: $1,081M contract drilling (YoY +19.3% vs $906M) — source: Transocean Q1 2026 release (deepwater.com), 2026-05-04
- EPS: GAAP diluted $0.06 (net income $71M, a swing from a $(79)M net loss a year ago) | Adjusted diluted loss $(0.03) (missed ~$0.07 est)
- One-line takeaway: Revenue beat and a swing to GAAP profit on much higher utilization; still an adjusted loss, but backlog ($7.1B), day rates (record ~$476k), and debt reduction all point the right way.

## 2. What happened this quarter
Utilization was the story. Total fleet utilization jumped to 86.7% (from 63.4% a year ago and 85.8% in Q4 2025), with ultra-deepwater at 82.1% and harsh-environment at 100%. That drove contract drilling revenue up 19% YoY to $1.081B and average daily revenue to $475,600 — the highest in over a decade — reflecting the tight high-spec offshore rig market. Adjusted EBITDA was $440M at a healthy 40.7% margin.

GAAP net income was $71M ($0.06/sh), a clear swing from the prior-year loss. But on an adjusted basis Transocean still posted a $(28)M loss ($(0.03)/sh), missing the consensus profit — the gap between GAAP and adjusted (and the market's ~8% negative reaction) reflects that day-rate strength is still being partly offset by interest costs on a heavy debt load. This is a recovering, not yet comfortably profitable, business.

## 3. Guidance & management commentary
CEO Keelan Adamson reiterated the multi-year upcycle thesis — "we are in the early days of a multi-year upcycle" for offshore services. Backlog underpins it: ~$7.1B as of May 4, with five new fixtures (~$1.6B) added since the February fleet status at a weighted-average day rate of ~$410,000. Tone: confident on the cycle. The strategic priority remains converting backlog and day-rate strength into free cash flow and deleveraging.

## 4. Balance sheet & capital allocation
- Total debt principal reduced to $5,137M (from $5,686M at year-end 2025) — ~$549M of deleveraging, the key value driver for equity holders.
- Total liquidity $1.125B.
- No dividend/buyback (cash is going to debt reduction) — appropriate given leverage.
- Leverage remains the central risk: ~$5.1B debt against $440M quarterly adjusted EBITDA (~$1.76B annualized) ≈ [EST] ~2.9x, still elevated.

## 5. Thesis check
- Supports the thesis: the offshore-recovery/deleveraging story is intact — record day rates, 86.7% utilization, growing backlog, and ~$0.5B of debt paydown in the quarter.
- Risk: still adjusted-loss-making; the equity is a leveraged call on offshore day rates. Any softening in oil/offshore demand hits both day rates and the ability to delever. Interest expense is the drag between EBITDA strength and bottom-line profit.
- Valuation: not sourced. [EST] Equity value is highly sensitive to EBITDA×multiple less ~$5.1B debt; the bull case is continued backlog conversion + deleveraging.

## 6. Watch items for next quarter (Q2 2026)
1. Backlog additions and day rates in the next fleet status — does the ~$410k/day fixture trend hold or rise?
2. Debt reduction pace and any refinancing.
3. Adjusted (not just GAAP) profitability — does the fleet cross into sustained adjusted profit?
