## 2. What happened this quarter
Lotus Creek is a small, early-stage light-oil producer developing Belly River wells at Wilson Creek, Alberta, and it is in aggressive growth mode. In Q1 2026 it drilled, completed, equipped, and brought onstream 3.0 gross (2.9 net) light-oil wells at an average cost of C$4.1M/well, investing C$14.4M of capital in the quarter — a large outlay relative to its size, reflecting a build-out phase. The company also expanded its capital budget alongside the Q1 report, signaling confidence in well results.

The relevant frame isn't quarterly profit (the company is spending to grow) but the production ramp: for context, October 2025 production had already jumped to 2,923 boe/d (+119% month-over-month) with monthly adjusted funds flow of C$2.7M (~C$32M annualized) — so the wells are delivering. Detailed Q1 2026 production/revenue/funds-flow figures weren't captured this run and should be confirmed from the release/SEDAR+.

## 3. Guidance & management commentary
This is the clearest part of the story. The 2026 budget is C$42.0M, targeting ~75% annual production growth, with drilling weighted to H2 2026, forecast Q4 2026 average production of ~4,000 boe/d, and production expected to peak in Q1 2027. Roughly C$33.6M (80%) is directed at drilling 6.0 gross light-oil Belly River wells plus expanding the 02-29 oil battery/gathering system (5,000 boe/d capacity). Tone: aggressive growth. The thesis is a rapid production/cash-flow ramp over 2026 into 2027.

## 4. Balance sheet & capital allocation
- Capital allocation is 100% growth: C$42M budget vs. ~C$32M annualized funds flow (as of late 2025) implies outspending cash flow — so the balance sheet/financing (debt draw or equity) is the key risk to confirm [not captured this run].
- No dividend/buyback (growth-stage).

## 5. Thesis check
- Growth/execution thesis: value depends almost entirely on hitting the production ramp (to ~4,000 boe/d) and the wells' economics, not on current results.
- Risks: outspending cash flow (financing/dilution or debt risk), single-play/single-area concentration (Belly River, Wilson Creek), oil-price exposure, well-performance/decline risk, and TSXV micro-cap liquidity. This is a high-risk, early-stage E&P.
- Valuation: not sourced. [EST] A pre-scale junior valued on forward production/cash-flow potential; highly sensitive to execution and oil prices. Confirm balance sheet before assessing.
