## 2. What happened this quarter
Rental and other property revenue rose to $19.7M (from $15.9M), reflecting portfolio growth in single-family rentals. But total expenses climbed to $26.2M — driven by property operating costs, depreciation & amortization, weather-related losses, and impairments on held-for-sale units — producing a total net loss of $10.3M and a net loss attributable to common of $3.4M, or $(0.90)/share. As with most externally-managed SFR REITs at this scale, GAAP EPS is depressed by D&A and non-cash items; NAV and FFO/AFFO are the metrics that matter, and those weren't captured in this run [not disclosed here].

Capital recycling continued: BHM sold 131 single-family units for ~$22.0M (~$17.2M net proceeds, a small $0.6M gain), consistent with active portfolio management.
