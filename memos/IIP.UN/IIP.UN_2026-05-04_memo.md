# InterRent REIT (IIP.UN) — Earnings Memo
Period: Q1 FY2026 | Report Date: 2026-05-04 | Prepared: 2026-07-13
Figures in C$. ⚠ See corporate note — InterRent was taken private on 2026-07-09.

## 1. Snapshot
- Revenue proxy: same-property proportionate NOI $38.9M (+1.0% YoY); NOI margin 64.1% — source: InterRent Q1 2026 results, 2026-05-04
- FFO/unit: $0.05 (down from $0.15) — depressed by $13.3M one-time privatization transaction costs
- One-line takeaway: ⚠ Largely moot as an ongoing investment — InterRent's $4B all-cash privatization at $13.55/unit (CLV Group + GIC) COMPLETED 2026-07-09; the REIT is being delisted. Q1 operations were steady; the headline FFO drop is deal-cost noise.

## 2. What happened this quarter
Operationally, a stable multifamily quarter. Occupancy was 96.3% (down 50 bps YoY), average monthly rent rose to $1,767 (+2.6% total, +1.5% same-property), and same-property proportionate NOI grew 1.0% to $38.9M at a steady 64.1% margin. InterRent captured a 3.1% gain-on-lease across 414 new leases — modest, reflecting a cooler Canadian rental market than the double-digit mark-to-market of prior years.

The headline FFO collapse — FFO/unit of $0.05 versus $0.15 a year ago, total FFO down 68% — looks alarming but is almost entirely a one-time item: $13.3M of transaction costs tied to the Arrangement Agreement (the privatization). Underlying operating FFO was far more stable than the reported number suggests.

## 3. Guidance & management commentary
There is no meaningful forward "guidance" because the entity has been acquired. The dominant fact is corporate: InterRent agreed to be acquired by Carriage Hill Properties Acquisition Corp. (CLV Group in partnership with GIC) in a ~$4B all-cash deal at $13.55/unit (a ~35% premium to the pre-announcement price), by statutory plan of arrangement. That transaction obtained Investment Canada Act approval and **completed on July 9, 2026**, after which InterRent is delisted from the TSX and ceases to be a reporting issuer.

## 4. Balance sheet & capital allocation
- Not a going concern in public-market terms post-close; net debt was assumed as part of the ~$4B transaction value.
- No further distributions/DRIP relevant post-privatization; unitholders receive $13.55/unit in cash.

## 5. Thesis check
