# Monument Mining Limited (MMY) — Earnings Memo
Period: Q3 FY2026 (qtr ended 2026-03-31) | Report Date: 2026-06-01 | Prepared: 2026-07-13
Figures in US$. Listing: TSXV. Note: fiscal year ends June 30.

## 1. Snapshot
- Revenue: US$47.04M gross (Q3) — source: Monument Q3 FY2026 release, 2026-06-01
- EPS: US$0.07 (Q3 net profit US$22.65M); YTD (9M) US$0.15 (net profit US$53.31M)
- One-line takeaway: Another strongly profitable quarter for the Selinsing gold mine — high gold prices driving fat margins (cash cost ~$1,390/oz), a fortress cash balance of ~US$102M, and a special dividend already paid. A cash-generative micro-cap gold producer.

## 2. What happened this quarter
Monument's Selinsing mine in Malaysia produced 11,700 oz in Q3 at a cash cost of US$1,390/oz. With gold prices far above that, gross revenue was US$47.04M and net profit was US$22.65M ($0.07/sh), bringing year-to-date (nine-month) net profit to US$53.31M ($0.15/sh) — very strong absolute profitability for a company of this size. The story is simple and favorable: steady production plus a high gold price equals large free cash flow.

That cash flow is visible on the balance sheet: cash and short-term deposits rose to US$101.76M (US$11.16M cash + US$90.60M term deposits) — a cash pile that is large relative to Monument's market cap and gives it optionality for growth (notably the Murchison Gold Project in Western Australia) and further shareholder returns.

## 3. Guidance & management commentary
No formal production guidance; management (CEO Cathy Zhai) framed the quarter around strong cash generation and directed exploration toward "resource expansion and mine life extension." The strategic questions are (1) Selinsing mine life (a small, mature deposit — hence the exploration emphasis) and (2) advancing the Murchison Gold Project (Gabanintha/Burnakura) in Australia as the next growth leg. Tone: confident, cash-focused.

## 4. Balance sheet & capital allocation
- Cash & term deposits ~US$101.76M, effectively net cash — a very strong position for a micro-cap.
- Capital return: a special dividend was announced Dec 11, 2025 and paid Jan 19, 2026 — evidence management will return excess cash, not just hoard it.
- The large cash balance is both a strength (downside protection, growth funding) and a capital-allocation question (deploy into Murchison, more dividends, or M&A?).

## 5. Thesis check
- Supports the thesis: high gold prices are converting Selinsing's steady output into outsized profit and cash; the balance sheet is pristine and management has shown willingness to pay special dividends.
- Risks: gold-price dependence; Selinsing mine-life/reserve depletion (key long-term risk); execution/permitting on Murchison; single-mine operating concentration; TSXV micro-cap liquidity.
- Valuation: not sourced. [EST] With ~US$102M cash and ~US$53M YTD net profit, the market cap is likely a low multiple of earnings net of cash; value hinges on gold prices and Selinsing mine-life extension.

## 6. Watch items for next quarter (Q4 FY2026, ends 2026-06-30)
1. Selinsing production/cash cost and any reserve/mine-life update from exploration.
2. Murchison Gold Project development progress (the growth catalyst).
3. Capital allocation — further special dividends vs. deploying the ~US$102M cash.
