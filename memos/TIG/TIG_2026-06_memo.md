# Team Internet Group plc (TIG) — Earnings Memo
Period: FY2025 results + 2026 YTD trading update | Report Date: FY25 ~2026-04 / YTD update ~2026-06 [dates approximate] | Prepared: 2026-07-13
Figures in US$. Listing: AIM:TIG (OTCQX: TIGXF).

## 1. Snapshot
- Revenue: FY2025 gross revenue $481.9M, net revenue $136.2M (28.3% gross margin) — source: TIG FY2025 results
- Earnings: FY2025 adjusted EBITDA $42.7M, but operating loss $49.9M and loss after tax $62.5M (driven by $41.7M impairments)
- One-line takeaway: The Search-monetization business is under structural pressure (big FY25 impairments and losses), but the DIS and Comparison segments are growing fast (~mid-teens net revenue, ~40% EBITDA growth); 2026 YTD trading is "in line," and a DIS-disposal strategic review is live.

## 2. What happened
FY2025 was a tale of two businesses. The legacy Search segment (Google AdSense-for-Domains / search-monetization) deteriorated, forcing $41.7M of impairment charges and pushing the group to an operating loss of $49.9M and a loss after tax of $62.5M — despite adjusted EBITDA of $42.7M on net revenue of $136.2M. The gap between positive adjusted EBITDA and large statutory losses is the impairment write-down of Search-related goodwill/assets.

The 2026 year-to-date trading update (five months ended May 31, 2026) shows stabilization: gross revenue $148M, net revenue $50M, adjusted EBITDA $16M, described as in line with expectations. Crucially, the DIS (domains/enterprise) and Comparison segments — which are unaffected by the Search transition — delivered mid-teens net revenue growth and ~40% EBITDA growth YoY. So the growth engines are performing; the drag is the shrinking, structurally-challenged Search business.

## 3. Guidance & management commentary
Management guided FY26 year-to-date trading in line with expectations and is pursuing value-unlocking moves: a strategic review of a potential disposal of the DIS segment, with an outcome expected in the first half of Q3 2026. Debt was refinanced during the period. Tone: focused on separating the growth assets (DIS/Comparison) from the declining Search business to crystallize value. Note: earlier takeover interest (Verdane and TowerBrook, ~125p/share cash, January 2025) did NOT proceed — Verdane withdrew in March 2025 — so there is no live offer currently.
