## 1. Snapshot
- Revenue: $49.8M (YoY +17%) — source: SandRidge Q1 2026 release, ~2026-05-06 [via release summary]
- EPS: GAAP $0.51 (net income $18.7M) | Adjusted $0.59 (adjusted net income $21.6M)
- One-line takeaway: Solid quarter — oil production +31%, revenue +17%, debt-free with $104M cash, and a raised regular dividend plus a special — the return-of-capital story intact.

## 2. What happened this quarter
Production averaged 18.6 MBoe/d (+4% YoY), but the mix improved meaningfully: oil production grew 31% YoY, lifting realizations and driving total revenue up 17% to $49.8M. Net income was $18.7M ($0.51/sh) with adjusted net income of $21.6M ($0.59/sh), and adjusted EBITDA of $33.7M. The shift toward higher-value oil (from a historically gassy Mid-Continent base) is the operational thread to watch.

The balance sheet remains a fortress for a small-cap E&P: zero debt and $104.1M of cash. That underpins a flexible, opportunistic capital-return posture rather than a fixed obligation.

## 3. Guidance & management commentary
No formal detailed guidance captured this run. The clearest signal was capital allocation: the Board raised the regular quarterly dividend 8% to $0.13/share and declared a one-time special dividend of $0.20/share (both payable June 1, record May 20). Tone: confident, return-focused — funding growing dividends from cash flow while keeping zero leverage. Strategic emphasis on oil-weighted development and potential accretive acquisitions given the cash pile.

## 4. Balance sheet & capital allocation
- Zero debt; $104.1M cash — exceptional flexibility.
- Dividend: $0.13 regular (+8%) + $0.20 special this quarter; special dividends are the swing lever tied to cash/commodity strength.
- No leverage or liquidity concerns; the question is deployment of the cash (buybacks/M&A/specials).

## 5. Thesis check
- Supports the thesis: debt-free, growing oil cut, rising dividend + specials — the low-risk, cash-return small-cap E&P story is working.
- Risk: commodity price exposure (especially oil differentials/gas prices) and small scale/single-region concentration; specials are discretionary and would be cut first if prices fall.
- Valuation: not sourced. [EST] Trades like a cash-rich, low-decline income E&P; value hinges on sustained oil prices and how the $104M cash is deployed.
