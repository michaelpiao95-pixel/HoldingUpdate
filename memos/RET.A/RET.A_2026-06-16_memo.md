## 1. Snapshot
- Revenue: $160.1M net revenues (YoY +0.8%; comparable sales +0.3%, store revenue +2.9%) — source: Reitmans Q1 results, 2026-06-16
- Earnings: adjusted EBITDA $(5.4)M — improved $5.2M YoY (Q1 is a seasonal loss quarter for apparel); gross margin stable at 55.7%
- One-line takeaway: Low-growth but improving — modest sales growth, stable high gross margin, and a materially smaller seasonal Q1 loss; the steady, cash-rich apparel-retail turnaround continues.

## 2. What happened this quarter
Q1 (the seasonally weakest quarter for apparel retail) showed slow-but-steady progress. Net revenues rose 0.8% to $160.1M, comparable sales edged up 0.3% (store revenue a firmer +2.9%, offset by softer e-commerce), and gross margin held at a healthy 55.7%. The clearest improvement was profitability: adjusted EBITDA of $(5.4)M was $5.2M better than a year ago — so the seasonal loss narrowed meaningfully, pointing to cost discipline and better full-price selling. For full-year context, FY2026 (ended Jan 31, 2026) had net revenues of $776.8M (+0.4%) with comparable sales down 0.7% — i.e., a roughly flat top line overall.

This is a mature, low-growth retailer executing on margins and cost rather than growth — the investment appeal (if any) rests on the balance sheet and valuation, not the P&L trajectory.

## 3. Guidance & management commentary
No formal guidance captured. Reitmans' post-restructuring story (it exited CCAA creditor protection years ago) is about disciplined operations across its Reitmans, Penningtons, and RW&CO. banners and a strong balance sheet. Tone read: steady. The key strategic question for holders remains capital allocation given the net-cash position (special dividends? buybacks? reinvestment?).

## 5. Thesis check
- Neutral-to-mildly-supportive: improving seasonal EBITDA and stable 55.7% gross margin are constructive, but ~flat sales cap the upside; this is a value/balance-sheet story, not growth.
- Risks: discretionary Canadian apparel demand, tariff/import costs, e-commerce softness, and the dual-class/controlled-company structure (RET.A is the non-voting class); capital-return policy is management's discretion.
