# NACCO Industries Inc. (NC) — Earnings Memo
Period: Q1 FY2026 | Report Date: 2026-05-05 | Prepared: 2026-07-13

## 1. Snapshot
- Revenue: $62.8M (YoY -4%) — source: NACCO Q1 2026 release, 2026-05-05
- EPS: GAAP diluted $1.17 (net income $8.8M, +80% YoY vs $0.66) — no separate adjusted EPS
- One-line takeaway: Profit surged despite lower revenue — margin expansion across Utility Coal, Contract Mining, and Minerals & Royalties lifted operating profit +43% and EBITDA +28%; growth capex ramping.

## 2. What happened this quarter
A textbook "lower revenue, higher profit" quarter driven by mix and cost. Consolidated revenue slipped 4% to $62.8M, but gross profit rose 48% to $14.3M and operating profit 43% to $11.0M, lifting net income 80% to $8.8M ($1.17/sh). Adjusted EBITDA grew 28% to $16.4M. The drivers were segment-specific: Utility Coal Mining benefited from lower costs at MLMC and the absence of a prior-year $3.0M inventory impairment; Contract Mining operating profit improved to $4.0M on higher tonnage, new dragline work, and a shift to units-of-production depreciation; and Minerals & Royalties held strong profitability despite lower revenue (lower depletion plus $1.0M of earnings from Eiger Resources).

The one caution some observers flagged is that part of the profit improvement is cost/accounting-driven (depreciation method change, absence of a prior impairment) rather than pure top-line growth — so the quality of the beat is mixed.

## 3. Guidance & management commentary
Management highlighted strength in Utility Coal and Contract Mining and is investing for growth: capital spending was $33M in Q1 with up to $57M planned for 2026 — a meaningful ramp for a company this size, signaling confidence in the Minerals & Royalties and mining-services growth strategy. Tone: constructive, growth-investment-oriented. NACCO's ongoing pivot is toward Minerals & Royalties (oil/gas/mineral interests) and diversified contract mining, reducing reliance on legacy thermal coal.

## 4. Balance sheet & capital allocation
- Liquidity $102.7M against debt of $126.4M — modest net debt, manageable.
- Elevated growth capex (up to $57M in 2026) will consume cash but is aimed at the higher-return Minerals & Royalties/services segments.
- NACCO has historically paid a dividend and repurchased shares; specific Q1 figures not captured this run [not captured].

## 5. Thesis check
- Supports the thesis: the diversification story (away from declining thermal coal toward minerals/royalties and contract mining) is showing up in margin expansion and EBITDA growth.
- Risk: revenue is still declining; part of the profit jump is non-recurring/accounting-aided; thermal coal secular decline and customer concentration remain structural risks; growth capex must earn its return.
- Valuation: not sourced. [EST] NACCO typically trades at a low earnings multiple as a small-cap coal/minerals name; re-rating depends on Minerals & Royalties growth becoming the visible earnings driver.

## 6. Watch items for next quarter (Q2 2026)
1. Minerals & Royalties revenue/earnings trajectory (the intended growth engine).
2. Contract Mining tonnage and new dragline contributions; sustainability of margin gains.
3. Growth capex pace vs. free cash flow and returns on the 2026 investment.
