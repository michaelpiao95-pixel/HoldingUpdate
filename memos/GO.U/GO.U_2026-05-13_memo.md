## 2. What happened this quarter
GO Residential owns a concentrated portfolio of luxury Manhattan apartment buildings and is still in its early post-IPO reporting cadence, so the relevant benchmark is performance versus IPO forecast — and it beat on both NOI and FFO. Adjusted NOI of $33.7M topped the $32.8M forecast at a very high 72.8% margin (luxury Manhattan operating economics), and Adjusted FFO of $16.5M ($0.29/unit) came in ~10.6% ahead of plan. The operating metrics were excellent: committed occupancy of 99.0%, tenant retention ~70.5%, average monthly rent of $6,876/suite, and blended lease trade-outs of 10.7% — meaning renewals/new leases repriced up double digits, a sign of genuine pricing power in a tight Manhattan market.

## 3. Guidance & management commentary
The forward catalyst is external growth: GO announced four new acquisitions that roughly double the building count and add over 1,000 suites, expected to drive mid-single-digit FFO growth and mid-single-digit NAV growth on an "effectively leverage-neutral" basis. Tone: confident, growth-oriented — management is leaning into a strong Manhattan market and scaling the portfolio without adding proportional leverage. This is a meaningful strategic step for a young REIT.
