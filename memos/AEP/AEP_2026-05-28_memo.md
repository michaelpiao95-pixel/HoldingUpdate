## 2. What happened this quarter
Q1 is seasonally the slowest quarter for Atlas (a maker of roof trusses, wall panels, and engineered building products), and 2026's was worse than usual. Revenue fell to C$9.3M, gross profit was just C$273K, and normalized EBITDA was a C$(0.8)M loss. Management (CEO Hadi Abassi) attributed the weakness to "difficult market conditions in Ontario and British Columbia and more severe winter weather across much of Canada than in recent years" — i.e., a demand/weather-driven trough rather than a structural problem. Housing/construction softness in AEP's key provinces is the macro headwind.

The quarter is best read as a cyclical/seasonal low point, with the investment case resting on the second-half ramp and new capacity rather than Q1 results.

## 3. Guidance & management commentary
Management expects stronger performance in subsequent quarters, citing three drivers: (1) the launch of its first robotic truss facility in Clinton, Ontario (expected operational at the beginning of July 2026) — an automation/productivity step-change; (2) continued expansion in wall panels; and (3) M&A. Tone: acknowledging a tough quarter but confident on the back half. The robotic facility is the key strategic milestone — it's meant to lift margins and capacity in a labor-constrained industry.
