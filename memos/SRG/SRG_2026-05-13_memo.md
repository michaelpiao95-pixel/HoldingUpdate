# Seritage Growth Properties (SRG) — Earnings Memo
Period: Q1 FY2026 | Report Date: 2026-05-13 | Prepared: 2026-07-11

## 1. Snapshot
- Revenue: $2.05M (YoY down from $4.6M) — source: Seritage Q1 2026 operating results, 2026-05-13
- EPS: GAAP $(0.56) — net loss attributable to common $(31.3)M (driven by $20.4M of impairments)
- One-line takeaway: ⚠ Going-concern warning — the $50M term loan matures July 31, 2026 and cash ($58.8M) is disclosed as insufficient for ongoing obligations; a wind-down/liquidation story now hinging on refinancing and asset sales.

## 2. What happened this quarter
Seritage is in wind-down, so conventional "earnings" are largely irrelevant — the numbers reflect a shrinking asset base and impairments. Revenue fell to $2.05M (from $4.6M) as the portfolio contracts, and the company recorded a $(31.3)M net loss ($(0.56)/sh), driven by a $15.2M impairment on a consolidated property and a $5.2M other-than-temporary impairment on an unconsolidated entity. Critically, no property sales closed in Q1 2026 (versus $29.6M of gross proceeds in Q1 2025) — the monetization engine stalled this quarter, which matters acutely given the looming debt maturity.

The dominant disclosure is liquidity: management stated existing cash will not be sufficient to fund ongoing obligations (opex, G&A, debt service), and the $50M term loan maturing July 31, 2026 is now a current liability — raising "substantial doubt" about the company's ability to continue as a going concern.

## 3. Guidance & management commentary
No earnings "guidance" — this is a strategic-review/wind-down situation. Management is actively engaged in discussions to refinance the ~$50M of remaining corporate debt due end-July 2026, and continues to pursue asset sales (a sale after quarter-end lifted cash to $63.2M by May 14). Tone: defensive/urgent, centered on liquidity and refinancing. The equity outcome depends entirely on realized asset values versus liabilities and wind-down costs.

## 4. Balance sheet & capital allocation
- Cash: $58.8M (incl. $14.3M restricted) at 3/31; $63.2M at 5/14 post-sale.
- Debt: ~$50M term loan due 2026-07-31 (current) — the binary near-term risk.
- Book equity $299.9M. ⚠ A third-party analyst estimate pegs liquidation recovery to equity at ~$145–185M before wind-down costs, litigation reserves, and preferred liquidation preference — this is a SECONDARY estimate, not company-provided [UNVERIFIED]; treat as illustrative only.
- No dividend on common; capital return is not applicable in wind-down.

## 5. Thesis check
- Weakens / high-risk: this is a special-situation liquidation play, and this quarter added a going-concern flag plus a stalled sales pace right before a debt maturity — the risk profile deteriorated.
- Key risks: (1) refinancing/maturity of the July 31 term loan; (2) realized asset values vs. book; (3) preferred liquidation preference sitting ahead of common; (4) wind-down/litigation costs.
- Valuation: this is a sum-of-the-parts/NAV liquidation analysis, not an earnings multiple. The only figures here are book equity $299.9M and an unverified third-party recovery range of $145–185M to equity — do independent NAV work before acting.
