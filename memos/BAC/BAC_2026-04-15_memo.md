# Bank of America Corporation (BAC) — Earnings Memo
Period: Q1 FY2026 | Report Date: 2026-04-15 | Prepared: 2026-07-11

## 1. Snapshot
- Revenue: $30.3B (net of interest expense; YoY +7%) — source: BAC 1Q26 release/presentation, 2026-04-15
- EPS: GAAP diluted $1.11 (+25% YoY; beat ~$1.01 consensus) — net income $8.6B (+17% YoY)
- One-line takeaway: Clean beat — revenue +7%, EPS +25%, NII up 9% with FY NII guidance raised to 6–8%; markets revenue (equities best in 15 years) the standout. Positive operating leverage.

## 2. What happened this quarter
A strong, broad-based quarter. Net income rose 17% YoY to $8.6B and EPS jumped 25% to $1.11 (EPS outgrew net income on a ~2% lower share count from buybacks). Revenue grew 7% to $30.3B. The engine was twofold: net interest income climbed 9% YoY to $15.7B ($15.9B FTE) on deposit growth and fixed-rate asset repricing, and markets revenue was exceptional — equities trading +30% to $2.83B, described as the best quarter in 15 years — alongside strong investment banking and sales & trading. BofA delivered positive operating leverage (revenue growth outpacing expense growth).

Credit remained well-behaved: provision for credit losses of $1.3B (down from $1.5B a year ago), net charge-offs of $1.4B, and an NCO ratio of 0.48% (down from 0.54% in 1Q25, up slightly from 0.44% in 4Q25). Returns were solid: ROE 12.0%, ROTCE 16.0%.

## 3. Guidance & management commentary
The headline forward signal was management raising full-year 2026 NII growth guidance to 6–8% (from 5–7%) — a confident read on the rate/deposit backdrop and the maturing of the fixed-rate asset repricing tailwind. Tone was confident. No restructuring or leadership changes flagged. The strategic story is unchanged: deposit franchise scale ($2.0T), disciplined expenses, and capital return.

## 4. Balance sheet & capital allocation
- CET1 ratio 11.2% (preliminary) — ample capital.
- Deposits $2.0T (+3% YoY); loans $1.2T.
- Capital returned: $2.0B common dividends + $7.2B buybacks = ~$9.2B in the quarter — a heavy return pace that drives EPS growth above net-income growth.
- Book value per share $38.66 (+7% YoY); tangible book value per share $28.84 (+7%).

## 5. Thesis check
- Supports the thesis: NII inflection (guidance raised), record-ish markets revenue, benign credit, and aggressive capital return — the core "rate-normalization + operating leverage + buyback" story is playing out.
- Risk: markets/IB revenue is cyclical and won't repeat a +30% equities quarter every period; NII guidance assumes a supportive rate path. Watch CRE/consumer credit for any turn (none evident yet).
- Valuation: not sourced. [EST] At ROTCE 16% and TBVPS $28.84 growing 7%/yr with heavy buybacks, the compounding case is intact; P/TBV not quantified without a market quote.

## 6. Watch items for next quarter (Q2 2026, reports ~mid-July 2026)
1. NII trajectory vs. the raised 6–8% full-year guide.
2. Markets revenue durability after the standout equities quarter.
3. Buyback pace and CET1 — can BofA sustain ~$7B/quarter repurchases?
