# Bank of America Corporation (BAC) — Earnings Memo
Period: Q2 FY2026 | Report Date: 2026-07-14 | Prepared: 2026-07-14

## 1. Snapshot
- Revenue: $31.6B (YoY +15%, QoQ +4.3% from Q1 2026 $30.3B) — source: BAC 2Q26 presentation, 2026-07-14
- EPS: GAAP diluted $1.21 | No non-GAAP adjustment disclosed — vs. prior quarter $1.11 (+9% QoQ); consensus beat $0.08 (est. $1.13)
- Net income: $9.1B (+27% YoY, +6% QoQ from $8.6B)
- One-line takeaway: Strong beat on both EPS and revenue, led by record equities trading and surging IB fees; NII guidance raised to the upper end of 6–8%; operating leverage target lifted to 300–400 bps for full year.

---

## 2. What happened this quarter

Revenue growth was broad-based and accelerated from Q1. Net interest income (GAAP) of $16.0B ($16.2B FTE) rose 9% YoY and $0.3B QoQ, driven by continued deposit and loan growth (+8% and +1% average, respectively), one extra accrual day, and ongoing fixed-rate asset repricing — partially offset by lower loan yields. Noninterest income of $15.6B surged 22% YoY, driven by three engines firing simultaneously: equities sales and trading ($3.6B, +70% YoY, record), FICC ($3.5B excl. DVA, +9% YoY, described as strongest in 10+ years), and investment banking fees of $2.1B (excl. self-led, +50% YoY), which reflected strength across debt underwriting, equity underwriting, and advisory. Asset management fees rose 20% YoY on higher AUM balances in GWIM.

Expenses were well controlled relative to revenue growth. Noninterest expense of $18.6B rose 8% YoY (vs. revenue up 15%), generating 6.6% operating leverage — well above management's stated targets. The efficiency ratio improved to 59%, a 359 bp improvement over 2Q25 and 220 bps over 1Q26. Every business segment contributed positive operating leverage and improved efficiency.

Credit quality remained stable and continued to improve on a YoY basis. Net charge-offs of $1.4B were broadly flat to Q1 2026 but down from $1.5B in 2Q25. The NCO ratio improved to 0.47% from 0.55% a year ago. Consumer delinquencies (30+ and 90+ days) and the credit card charge-off rate of 3.55% improved both YoY and QoQ. Commercial criticized exposures fell $2.3B sequentially to $22.1B, and nonperforming loans declined $80MM from Q1 to $5.8B. The net reserve release of $46MM (vs. reserve build of $67MM in 2Q25) signals management's confidence in the credit environment.

Capital deployment was aggressive: the bank repurchased $6.0B in common stock and paid $2.0B in common dividends in the quarter, returning $8.0B to shareholders — slightly below Q1 2026's $9.2B pace but still substantial. The share count fell to 7.02B from 7.13B in Q1, amplifying per-share metrics; EPS rose 9% QoQ on a 6% gain in net income.

## 3. Guidance & management commentary

CEO Brian Moynihan: "The team delivered one of our strongest quarters to date, with earnings per share up 34% year-over-year. Every business segment reported double digit net income growth and strong returns on equity."

Tone was constructive. Management raised two key forward metrics:
- **Full-year 2026 NII growth:** Now expected at the **upper end of the 6–8% range** (previously guided to 6–8% in April 2026, upgraded from 5–7% in January). This guidance embeds one 25 bp rate hike in September 2026.
- **Full-year 2026 operating leverage:** Raised to **300–400 bps** from the previous 200+ bps target.

NII asset sensitivity as of June 30, 2026: a parallel +100 bp shift adds ~$1.0B to NII over 12 months; a -100 bp shift reduces NII by ~$2.2B. NIM target of 230 bps (vs. 2.08% currently) described as achievable within two years (previously guided as a 2–3 year goal). No specific Q3 2026 NII dollar figure was disclosed, but the sequential trend (+$0.3B QoQ) combined with continued repricing and loan growth implies a constructive Q3 setup.

On the economic backdrop, BofA's own research estimates 2026 US GDP growth at 2.2% and 2026 global growth at 3.2%, with consumer spending in 1H26 running +5% vs. 1H25 in dollar volume. No recession flag. Moynihan described the consumer as "more durable than expected" and cited "full pipelines" in markets, business, and consumer segments.

Strategic emphasis on AI: 200K+ employees using AI tools generating 400K+ daily prompts, 300+ approved use cases, 114 live generative AI cases. Management framed this as driving efficiency in call centers, advisory workflows, and developer productivity — a structural cost-curve tailwind over the medium term.

## 5. Thesis check

The investment thesis for BAC — rate normalization driving NII recovery, positive operating leverage narrowing the efficiency gap vs. peers, and aggressive buybacks compounding per-share value — advanced materially this quarter:

- **NII inflection confirmed:** $16.0B GAAP NII is up from $14.7B in 2Q25 and trending toward the guided upper-end of the 6–8% full-year growth target. Fixed-rate asset repricing (the HTM maturity tailwind) continues.
- **Operating leverage story intact:** 6.6% in Q2, 4.7% in 1H26. Full-year guide raised to 300–400 bps.
- **Credit benign:** NCO ratio of 0.47%, criticized exposures declining, no signs of consumer stress. The reserve release (however small) is a confidence signal.
- **Markets revenue durability question (prior watch item — partially resolved):** Equities at $3.6B (+70% YoY) is a record; FICC at $3.5B is the strongest in 10+ years. This exceeded Q1 2026's already-elevated markets level. Some of the YoY gain reflects an exceptionally active market environment that may not repeat at the same magnitude, but the structural share-gain narrative (17 consecutive quarters of YoY S&T growth) supports durability above pre-2024 levels.
- **Buyback pace (prior watch item — maintained):** $6.0B in Q2 vs. $7.2B in Q1; CET1 held at 11.2% while returning $8B. The pace is sustainable given earnings generation.

**New risk to monitor:** The All Other segment reported a revenue drag of -$744MM (vs. -$21MM in Q1 and +$89MM in 2Q25), primarily ALM/rate positioning. This is a meaningful swing. If interest rates move against positioning, this drag could widen. Also, HTM portfolio at $506B remains an unrealized-loss overhang on AOCI (not CET1, but relevant to tangible book), though it continues to shrink with maturities.

**Valuation context [EST]:** At ROTCE of 17.0% and TBVPS of $29.37 growing ~7%/yr with $6B+/quarter buybacks, the compounding case is strong. Market price not sourced for this memo; P/TBV not quantified.

## 6. Watch items for next quarter (Q3 2026, reports ~mid-October 2026)

1. **NII trajectory vs. the "upper end of 6–8%" guide:** Q2 NII was $16.0B GAAP; sustaining this through Q3 with one more rate hike priced in requires deposit cost stability and continued repricing. Watch for any unexpected deposit outflows or rate pressure.
2. **Markets revenue normalization:** Q1 and Q2 2026 both set records in equities. A quarter without a macro catalyst (tariff uncertainty, rate volatility) could see a meaningful reversion. Monitor whether 17-quarter YoY S&T growth streak holds.
3. **All Other revenue drag and ALM positioning:** The -$744MM swing in All Other is worth tracking; if the macro rate path deviates from management's September-hike assumption, ALM mark-to-market effects could create earnings volatility.
